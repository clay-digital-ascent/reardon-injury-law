Pedestrian Accident Injuries & Cases We Handle

Pedestrian accidents almost always result in serious injuries. We help victims of:

Traumatic Brain Injuries

Pedestrians who are struck by vehicles often hit their head on the ground or the vehicle itself. TBIs can cause permanent cognitive impairment, memory loss, and personality changes.

Spinal Cord Injuries

The violent impact of a vehicle striking a pedestrian can cause spinal cord damage leading to partial or complete paralysis, requiring a lifetime of medical care and assistance.

Broken Bones & Fractures

Pedestrian accidents commonly cause fractured legs, hips, pelvis, and arms. Many require multiple surgeries with pins, plates, or rods and months of painful rehabilitation.

Road Rash & Lacerations

Being thrown onto pavement causes severe road rash, deep lacerations, and scarring that may require skin grafts and plastic surgery. These injuries cause lasting physical and emotional pain.

Internal Injuries

Internal bleeding and organ damage from blunt force trauma may not show symptoms immediately but can be life-threatening. Our medical expertise helps identify these hidden injuries early.

Wrongful Death

Pedestrian accidents are among the most deadly. When a loved one is killed by a negligent driver, we help families pursue wrongful death claims for funeral costs, lost income, and their devastating loss.

What to Do After a Pedestrian Accident

1

Call 911

Your safety comes first. Call 911 immediately to get emergency medical responders and police to the scene. A police report is critical evidence.

2

Get Witness Names and Photos

If you're able, get contact information from anyone who saw the accident. Take photos of the scene, the vehicle, and your injuries.

3

Seek Emergency Care

Even if you feel okay at the scene, get a full medical evaluation. Adrenaline masks pain, and internal injuries may not be immediately apparent.

4

Call Reardon Injury Law Before Speaking to Any Insurer

Insurance adjusters will contact you quickly. Do not give any statements before speaking with an attorney. We protect your rights from day one.

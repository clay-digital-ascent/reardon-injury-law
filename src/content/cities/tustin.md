## What to Do After an Accident in Tustin

The steps you take immediately following an accident are crucial for protecting both your health and your legal rights. If you are injured in Tustin, try to remain calm and follow this guide:

1.  **Get to Safety:** If you are able, move yourself and your vehicle out of the flow of traffic to a safe location.
2.  **Call 911:** Report the accident immediately. The Tustin Police Department will likely respond to incidents on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles crashes on freeways like the SR-55 or I-5. A police report is vital evidence.
3.  **Seek Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Accept medical care from paramedics and go to the emergency room if advised. Because Tustin does not have its own hospital, you will likely be transported to a major medical center in nearby Irvine or Santa ana. Even if you feel fine, some serious injuries have delayed symptoms.
4.  **Document the Scene:** If you are physically able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, and any visible injuries.
5.  **Exchange Information:** Get the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details from all other parties involved. Also, try to get contact information for any witnesses.
6.  **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid making statements like "I'm sorry" or "It was my fault." Stick to the facts when speaking with police and other drivers.
7.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you speak to any insurance adjusters, contact an experienced Tustin personal injury lawyer. An attorney can protect you from lowball settlement offers and handle all communications on your behalf so you can focus on healing.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system after an injury can be daunting. In California, several key laws will impact your claim.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your total award will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% responsible, your compensation will be reduced by 20%.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** For most personal injury cases in California, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If you miss this deadline, you will likely lose your right to seek compensation forever.
*   **Claims Against Government Entities:** The deadline is much shorter if your claim is against a government agency (e.g., if your accident was caused by a poorly maintained road owned by the City of Tustin or a public bus). Under the California Government Claims Act, you must file a formal claim within **six months** of the injury.
*   **Compensation You Can Recover:** A successful personal injury claim can provide compensation for economic and non-economic damages, including medical expenses (past and future), lost wages, loss of future earning capacity, property damage, and pain and suffering.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low amounts are often insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury, making it critical to identify all possible sources of recovery.

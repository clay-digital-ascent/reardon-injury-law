## What to Do After an Accident in Aliso Viejo

The steps you take immediately following an accident are crucial for both your health and your potential legal claim.

1.  **Prioritize Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle to a safe location off the roadway. Call 911 immediately. The Orange County Sheriff's Department will likely respond to an accident within Aliso Viejo city limits, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles incidents on the I-5 and I-405.
2.  **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Even if you feel fine, adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Request paramedics at the scene or go to a nearby emergency room, such as **Hoag Hospital Irvine** or **Mission Hospital Mission Viejo**, for a thorough evaluation. This creates a medical record linking your injuries to the accident.
3.  **Document the Scene:** Use your phone to take extensive photos and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle damage, skid marks, traffic signals, and any visible injuries. If there are witnesses, get their names and contact information.
4.  **Exchange Information and Avoid Admitting Fault:** Exchange driver's license, insurance, and contact information with the other driver(s). Be polite, but do not apologize or say anything that could be interpreted as an admission of fault.
5.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you give a recorded statement to any insurance company—especially the other driver’s—speak with a qualified personal injury lawyer. An attorney can protect your rights and handle all communications with the insurance adjusters on your behalf.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system after an injury can be overwhelming. An experienced attorney can guide you through California's specific laws to ensure you are treated fairly.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total compensation award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found 10% at fault, you can still collect 90% of your damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** In most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, a critical exception exists for claims against government bodies (e.g., a city, county, or state entity). Under the Government Claims Act, you have only **six months** to file a formal notice of claim. Missing this deadline can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
*   **California's Minimum Insurance:** The state's minimum liability insurance requirements are just $15,000 per person, $30,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $5,000 for property damage. In a serious accident, these low limits are often exhausted quickly. An attorney can help you identify all possible sources of recovery, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
*   **Available Compensation:** A successful personal injury claim can provide compensation (known as "damages") for both your economic and non-economic losses. This includes medical bills (past and future), lost wages, loss of future earning capacity, property damage, and compensation for your physical pain and emotional suffering.

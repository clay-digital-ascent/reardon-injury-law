## Accident Hotspots in Half Moon Bay

While Half Moon Bay is known for its scenic beauty, its roads present unique dangers for drivers, motorcyclists, and cyclists. The combination of local traffic, tourist congestion, and challenging road designs contributes to serious accidents every year. In 2022 alone, the city saw 44 fatal and injury collisions.

Some of the key areas and factors of concern include:

* **State Routes 1 and 92:** The Cabrillo Highway (CA-1) and CA-92 are the lifelines of the coastside. However, they are also prone to heavy congestion, especially on weekends and during the summer. This mix of slow-moving tourist traffic and local commuters creates a high-risk environment for rear-end collisions, sideswipes, and serious <a href="/car-accidents">car accidents</a>.
* **Unsignalized Intersections:** Many of Half Moon Bay’s arterial and collector roads feature unsignalized intersections. These crossings require a high degree of driver attention and can be hotspots for T-bone collisions and accidents involving <a href="/pedestrian-accidents">pedestrian accidents</a>, particularly when visibility is poor.
* **Speed and Nighttime Driving:** Local data shows that speed is a significant factor in many local crashes. Combined with the coastal fog and poorly lit rural stretches of road, driving at night significantly increases the risk of an accident.
* **Nearby Commute Corridors:** Many Half Moon Bay residents commute through neighboring cities. Major arterials like El Camino Real (CA-82) in cities like Daly City and South San Francisco are known for wide lanes and busy intersections that are frequently the site of serious collisions, including those involving <a href="/bicycle-accidents">bicycle accidents</a>.

## What to Do After an Accident in Half Moon Bay

The steps you take immediately following an accident are critical for protecting your health and your legal rights.

1. **Get to Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Call 911 to report the accident and any injuries. Depending on your location, the San Mateo County Sheriff's Office or the California Highway Patrol (CHP) will respond.
2. **Seek Immediate Medical Evaluation:** Your health is the top priority. Even if you feel okay, adrenaline can mask serious injuries. It is crucial to be evaluated by a medical professional. As there are no hospitals directly in Half Moon Bay, paramedics will likely transport you to the nearest emergency room, such as **San Mateo Medical Center** or **Sequoia Hospital** in Redwood City.
3. **Document the Scene:** If you are physically able, take photos and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle damage, skid marks, road conditions, and any visible injuries. Exchange contact and insurance information with the other driver(s) and get the names and phone numbers of any witnesses.
4. **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid saying things like "I'm sorry" or "It was my fault." Stick to the facts when speaking with law enforcement. Do not give a recorded statement to any insurance company, including your own, until you have spoken with an attorney.
5. **Contact an Injury Lawyer:** The insurance company's goal is to pay out as little as possible. An experienced personal injury lawyer will protect your rights, handle all communication with the insurers, and begin building a case to secure the compensation you need for your recovery.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires understanding a few key California laws.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your final award will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, your compensation will be reduced by 20%.
* **Statute of Limitations:** In most cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). This is a strict deadline. A critical exception is for claims against a government entity—such as the City of Half Moon Bay, San Mateo County, or the State of California. In these cases, you must file a formal claim within **six months** of the incident.
* **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry minimum liability coverage of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low amounts are often not enough to cover the costs of a serious injury. Our firm excels at identifying all potential sources of recovery, including uninsured/underinsured motorist policies and third-party liability.
* **Recoverable Damages:** A successful claim can provide compensation for both economic and non-economic losses, including medical bills (past and future), lost wages, loss of earning capacity, property damage, pain and suffering, and emotional distress. If your case proceeds to trial, it will be heard at the **Superior Court of California, County of San Mateo, Redwood City Courthouse**.

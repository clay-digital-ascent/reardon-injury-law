## What to Do After an Accident in Campbell

The moments after an accident are chaotic and stressful. Taking the right steps can protect your health and your legal rights.

1. **Prioritize Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Check on others involved. Call 911 immediately to report the accident and request medical assistance. The Campbell Police Department will likely respond to incidents on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles crashes on SR-17 and SR-85.
2. **Seek Medical Evaluation:** Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. It is crucial to be evaluated by a medical professional, even if you feel fine. For residents of Campbell, this may mean going to a nearby emergency room at **Good Samaritan Hospital** or the Level II Trauma Center at **Regional Medical Center of San Jose**. This creates an official record of your injuries, which is vital for your claim.
3. **Document Everything at the Scene:** If you are able, take photos and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, and any visible injuries. Exchange names, contact information, and insurance details with all other drivers involved. Be sure to get contact information for any witnesses.
4. **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid discussing who was at fault with anyone at the scene, including the other driver or even the police. Stick to the facts of what happened.
5. **Report the Crash and Preserve Evidence:** You can later obtain a copy of the official traffic collision report from the responding agency (Campbell PD or CHP). Be wary of speaking to the other party’s insurance adjuster, who may try to get you to make a recorded statement or accept a low settlement offer.
6. **Consult with an Experienced Attorney:** Before you agree to anything, contact a personal injury lawyer. An attorney can handle all communications with insurance companies, manage the legal deadlines, and ensure you are taking the right steps to build a strong case.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the aftermath of an injury requires understanding the specific laws that govern personal injury claims in California.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your final award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 10% at fault, you can still collect 90% of the total damages.
* **Statute of Limitations:** There are strict deadlines for filing a personal injury lawsuit. In most cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a claim (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, there is a critical exception: if your claim is against a government entity (like the City of Campbell or the State of California), you must file a formal notice of claim within **six months** of the incident. Missing these deadlines can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
* **Compensation for Your Losses:** Victims of negligence are entitled to seek compensation, known as "damages," for all of their losses. This can include:
 * **Economic Damages:** Tangible financial losses such as past and future medical bills, lost wages, diminished earning capacity, and property damage.
 * **Non-Economic Damages:** Intangible losses that compensate you for your pain and suffering, emotional distress, disfigurement, and loss of enjoyment of life.
* **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low limits are often insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury, making it essential to explore all possible sources of recovery with your attorney.

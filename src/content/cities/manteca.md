## What to Do After an Accident in Manteca

The steps you take immediately following an accident are critical for both your health and your potential legal claim.

1. **Ensure Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Call 911 immediately to report the crash and request emergency medical services. The Manteca Police Department typically responds to incidents on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles crashes on SR-99 and SR-120.
2. **Seek Medical Attention:** Accept medical help at the scene. Even if you feel you are not seriously hurt, it is vital to get a full medical evaluation. Adrenaline can mask pain from serious injuries. You can go to a local facility like **Doctors Hospital of Manteca** or **Kaiser Permanente Manteca Medical Center**. For severe, life-threatening injuries, you may be transported to the designated regional trauma center, **San Joaquin General Hospital**, in nearby French Camp.
3. **Document the Scene:** Use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, skid marks, traffic signals, and your injuries. If there are witnesses, get their names and contact information.
4. **Exchange Information:** Exchange driver's licenses, insurance information, and vehicle details with the other driver(s). Do not apologize or admit fault. Stick to the facts.
5. **File an Official Report:** The responding officers will create an accident report. Be sure to get the report number. This official document is a crucial piece of evidence for your claim.
6. **Contact an Attorney:** Before you speak with any insurance adjusters—even your own—contact a personal injury lawyer. The insurance company's goal is to minimize its payout. Having an experienced attorney on your side from the beginning protects your rights and levels the playing field.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system can be intimidating, but understanding a few key California laws is essential for any injury victim. If your case proceeds to trial, it will likely be heard at the **San Joaquin County Superior Court - Stockton Branch**.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California law allows you to recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total compensation award is simply reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 10% responsible for a crash, you can still collect 90% of your damages.
* **Statute of Limitations:** In most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, there is a critical exception: if your claim is against a government entity (e.g., a city-operated vehicle or a dangerous condition on public property), you must file a formal claim within just **six months** under the Government Claims Act. Missing this deadline can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
* **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of only $15,000 per person, $30,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $5,000 for property damage. In any serious accident, these amounts are often woefully inadequate to cover medical bills and other losses. This is why it is so important to identify all possible sources of recovery.
* **Recoverable Damages:** As an injury victim, you may be entitled to compensation for economic and non-economic losses, including:
 * Past and future medical expenses
 * Lost wages and reduced future earning capacity
 * Property damage
 * Pain, suffering, and emotional distress
 * Loss of enjoyment of life

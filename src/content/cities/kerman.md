## What to Do After an Accident in Kerman

The actions you take in the minutes and hours after an accident are critical for your health and your potential legal claim. Follow these steps to protect yourself:

1.  **Ensure Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle to a safe location off the roadway. Check on others involved and call 911 immediately. Report the accident and any known injuries. Depending on your location, the Kerman Police Department or the Fresno County Sheriff's Office will likely respond.
2.  **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Adrenaline can mask the symptoms of serious injuries like whiplash or internal bleeding. Even if you feel fine, get a medical evaluation. For severe trauma, you will likely be transported to a regional trauma center like **Community Regional Medical Center** or **Saint Agnes Medical Center** in Fresno.
3.  **Document the Scene:** Use your phone to take pictures and videos of everything: vehicle damage, skid marks, road conditions, traffic signals, and your injuries. This evidence can be invaluable later.
4.  **Exchange Information:** Get the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details of all drivers involved. Also, collect the names and phone numbers of any witnesses.
5.  **Avoid Admitting Fault:** Do not apologize or accept blame for the accident. Stick to the facts when speaking with law enforcement. Do not give a recorded statement to the other driver's insurance company before consulting with an attorney.
6.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you talk to insurance adjusters, speak with a personal injury lawyer. We can help you navigate the process, from filing an official crash report to handling all communications with insurers, ensuring your rights are protected from the very beginning.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system after an injury can be confusing. It is important to understand the key California laws that will affect your case.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 10% responsible, you can still collect 90% of your damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** There are strict deadlines for filing a lawsuit. For most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a claim (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, the deadline is much shorter for claims against a public entity. If your injury was caused by a government employee or a dangerous condition on public property (like a poorly maintained road), you must file a formal claim within **six months** under the Government Claims Act.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's mandatory liability insurance minimums are just $15,000 per person, $30,000 per accident, and $5,000 for property damage. These low amounts are often not enough to cover the costs of a serious injury. Our firm excels at identifying all potential sources of compensation, including underinsured/uninsured motorist policies and third-party liability.
*   **Potential Compensation:** As an injury victim, you may be entitled to recover compensation for a wide range of losses, including past and future medical expenses, lost wages and earning capacity, property damage, and non-economic damages like pain, suffering, and emotional distress.

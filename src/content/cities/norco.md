## What to Do After an Accident in Norco

The actions you take in the minutes and hours after an accident can significantly impact your health and your ability to file a successful injury claim. Follow these steps:

1.  **Ensure Your Safety:** If possible, move your vehicle to the side of the road to avoid further collisions, especially on busy roads like Hamner Avenue or the I-15. Turn on your hazard lights.
2.  **Call 911:** Report the accident immediately. The Riverside County Sheriff's Department typically responds to incidents on local streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles crashes on freeways like the I-15 and SR-91. A police report is a critical piece of evidence.
3.  **Seek Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Even if you feel fine, some serious injuries have delayed symptoms. Allow paramedics to evaluate you at the scene and be sure to get a full check-up at a hospital like **Corona Regional Medical Center** or, for more severe trauma, **Riverside University Health System Medical Center**.
4.  **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, traffic signals, and any visible injuries.
5.  **Exchange Information:** Get the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details of all other drivers involved. Also, collect contact information from any witnesses.
6.  **Avoid Admitting Fault:** Do not apologize or say the accident was your fault. Stick to the facts when speaking with police and other drivers. Any admission of guilt can be used against you later by insurance companies.
7.  **Contact an Experienced Norco Injury Attorney:** Before you speak with an insurance adjuster, call Reardon Injury Law. Insurance companies aim to minimize their payouts. We will protect your rights and handle all communications on your behalf to ensure you are not taken advantage of.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires an understanding of specific California laws. An experienced attorney can guide you through these complexities, but here are some key principles that may apply to your Norco injury case:

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your final compensation award will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, you can still collect 80% of the total damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** In California, you generally have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If you miss this deadline, you will likely lose your right to seek compensation forever.
*   **Government Claims Act:** A critical exception to the two-year rule applies if your claim is against a government entity—such as the City of Norco, Riverside County, or the State of California (e.g., an accident caused by a city vehicle or a dangerous condition on a public road). In these cases, you must file a formal claim within **six months** of the incident.
*   **California's Minimum Insurance Requirements:** The state requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of only $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. In any serious accident, these amounts are rarely enough to cover all the costs. We can help you explore all available sources of recovery, including your own uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage.

Victims of negligence are entitled to seek compensation for medical expenses (past and future), lost wages, loss of future earning capacity, and non-economic damages like pain, suffering, and emotional distress.

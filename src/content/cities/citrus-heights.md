## What to Do After an Accident in Citrus Heights

The steps you take immediately following an accident are crucial for protecting both your health and your legal rights.

1.  **Prioritize Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle to a safe location off the main roadway. Call 911 immediately. This will dispatch the Citrus Heights Police Department (CHPD) and emergency medical services to the scene.
2.  **Seek Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Allow paramedics to evaluate you at the scene. Even if you feel fine, it is essential to get a full medical evaluation at an urgent care clinic or an emergency room, such as nearby Mercy San Juan Medical Center or Sutter Roseville Medical Center. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries that may only become apparent hours or days later.
3.  **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures of the accident scene, including the positions of the vehicles, property damage, skid marks, traffic signals, and your injuries. Exchange names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details with all other drivers involved.
4.  **File an Official Report:** The responding CHPD officer will create a Traffic Collision Report. This report is a critical piece of evidence. Make sure you get the officer's name and the report number so you can obtain a copy later.
5.  **Avoid Admitting Fault:** Do not apologize or admit any fault for the accident to anyone, including the other driver or police. Stick to the facts of what happened. Do not give a recorded statement to the other party’s insurance company without first speaking to an attorney.
6.  **Contact a Personal Injury Lawyer:** Before you accept any settlement offer, contact an experienced attorney. An attorney can protect you from aggressive insurance adjusters and ensure you are pursuing the full compensation you are legally entitled to.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires an understanding of key California laws. An experienced attorney can apply these laws to the specifics of your case.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your total compensation will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, you can still collect 80% of your damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** In California, you generally have **two years** from the date of your injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, there is a critical exception: if your claim is against a government entity (like the City of Citrus Heights or the State of California), you must file a formal administrative claim within just **six months** of the incident. Missing these deadlines can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of only $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low amounts are often completely inadequate to cover the costs of a serious injury. An attorney can help identify all potential sources of recovery, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
*   **Available Compensation (Damages):** Victims of negligence are entitled to seek compensation for economic and non-economic losses, including medical bills (past and future), lost wages, loss of future earning capacity, property damage, and pain and suffering.

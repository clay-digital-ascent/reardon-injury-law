## What to Do After an Accident in Monrovia

The actions you take immediately following an accident are critical for protecting your health and your legal rights. If you are involved in a collision in Monrovia, follow these steps:

1.  **Prioritize Safety:** If possible, move your vehicle out of traffic to a safe location. Turn on your hazard lights to alert other drivers.
2.  **Call 911:** Report the accident to the authorities. The Monrovia Police Department typically responds to incidents on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles crashes on the I-210 and I-605 freeways.
3.  **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Even if you feel fine, it is essential to get a medical evaluation. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Paramedics can assess you at the scene, and you should follow up by visiting an emergency room at a nearby facility like **Methodist Hospital of Southern California** in Arcadia or **Huntington Hospital** in Pasadena, which serves as a major trauma center for the region.
4.  **Document the Scene:** Use your phone to take photos and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, traffic signals, and any visible injuries.
5.  **Exchange Information:** Collect the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details from all drivers involved. Also, get the names and phone numbers of any witnesses.
6.  **Do Not Admit Fault:** Be polite, but do not apologize or accept blame for the accident. Stick to the facts when speaking with police and other parties.
7.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you give a recorded statement to an insurance adjuster, contact Reardon Injury Law. We can advise you on how to protect your rights and handle all communications with the insurance companies on your behalf.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires a clear understanding of California law. Here are some key principles that may affect your case:

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% responsible, you can still collect 80% of the damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** Time is of the essence. In California, you have a **two-year statute of limitations** to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, a critical exception exists for claims against government entities (e.g., a city bus, a poorly maintained public road). In these cases, you must file a formal claim within just **six months** of the injury. Missing this deadline can permanently bar you from seeking compensation.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low amounts are often insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury. An experienced attorney can help you identify all possible sources of recovery, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
*   **Available Compensation:** A successful claim can help you recover compensation for both economic and non-economic losses, including medical bills, future medical care, lost wages, diminished earning capacity, property damage, pain and suffering, and emotional distress.

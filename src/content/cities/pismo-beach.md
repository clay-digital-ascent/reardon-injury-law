## What to Do After an Accident in Pismo Beach

The steps you take immediately following an accident are crucial for protecting your health and your legal rights. If you are injured in Pismo Beach, try to follow this guide:

1.  **Prioritize Safety**: Move to a safe location away from traffic if possible. Check on others involved and render aid if you are able.
2.  **Contact Emergency Services**: Call 911 immediately. The Pismo Beach Police Department will likely respond to accidents on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles incidents on Highway 101. A police report is vital evidence for your claim.
3.  **Seek Medical Attention**: Even if you feel fine, it is essential to get a medical evaluation. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. The nearest major hospitals equipped to handle trauma are **Sierra Vista Regional Medical Center** and **French Hospital Medical Center**, both located in San Luis Obispo. A documented medical visit creates a direct link between the accident and your injuries.
4.  **Document Everything**: Use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, and any visible injuries. Exchange contact and insurance information with the other parties involved, but do not discuss fault.
5.  **Gather Witness Information**: If there are any witnesses, ask for their names and phone numbers. Their independent testimony can be invaluable.
6.  **Do Not Speak to Insurance Adjusters**: Before giving a recorded statement to any insurance company—especially the at-fault party's—contact an attorney. Adjusters are trained to minimize payouts, and your words can be used against you.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

California has specific laws that govern personal injury claims. Understanding these rules is key to protecting your right to compensation.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence**: California follows a "pure comparative negligence" system. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at-fault for the accident. For example, if you were found to be 20% responsible, your total compensation award would simply be reduced by 20%. Insurance companies often try to assign victims a higher percentage of fault to reduce their liability, which is why having an attorney to defend you is critical.
*   **Statute of Limitations**: Time is of the essence. Under California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1, you generally have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit. However, there is a much shorter deadline for claims against government entities. If your injury was caused by a public entity (e.g., a dangerous road condition, a city-operated vehicle), you must file a formal claim within **six months** under the Government Claims Act.
*   **California's Minimum Insurance Requirements**: California law only requires drivers to carry minimum liability coverage of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low limits are often insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury. An experienced attorney can help identify all potential sources of compensation, including your own Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
*   **Types of Compensation**: A successful personal injury claim can help you recover damages for medical expenses (past and future), lost wages, loss of future earning capacity, property damage, and non-economic damages like pain, suffering, and emotional distress.

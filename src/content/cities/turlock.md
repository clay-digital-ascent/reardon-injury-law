## What to Do After an Accident in Turlock

The steps you take immediately following an accident are critical for protecting your health and your legal rights.

1. **Prioritize Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Check on yourself and others for injuries. Call 911 immediately to report the crash and request medical assistance. The Turlock Police Department will likely respond to accidents within city limits, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles incidents on SR-99 and other state highways.
2. **Seek Medical Evaluation:** Accept medical help at the scene and get a full evaluation at a hospital or urgent care clinic, even if you feel fine. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. In Turlock, you may be transported to a major medical facility within Stanislaus County for comprehensive care. This creates an official record of your injuries.
3. **Document the Scene:** Use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle positions, property damage, skid marks, road conditions, and your visible injuries.
4. **Gather Information:** Collect the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details from all drivers involved. Also, get the names and phone numbers of any witnesses.
5. **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid saying "I'm sorry" or admitting any level of fault. Stick to the facts when speaking with law enforcement. Do not give a recorded statement to the other party's insurance company without first consulting an attorney.
6. **Contact an Injury Lawyer:** Call Reardon Injury Law for a free consultation. We can immediately advise you on how to protect your claim, handle communications with insurance adjusters, and begin building your case.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system can be intimidating, but understanding your basic rights is the first step toward securing justice. In California, several key laws protect injury victims.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California law allows you to recover damages even if you were partially to blame for the accident. Your total compensation will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, you can still collect 80% of the total damages.
* **Statute of Limitations:** There are strict deadlines for filing a personal injury claim. For most cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). The clock can tick quickly, so it is vital to act fast.
* **Government Claims:** If your injury was caused by a public entity—such as a Turlock city bus, a poorly maintained public road, or a government employee—you must file a formal notice of claim within **six months** of the incident. Missing this short deadline can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
* **Compensation You Can Recover:** As a victim, you may be entitled to compensation (known as "damages") for a variety of losses, including:
 * Past and future medical expenses
 * Lost wages and loss of future earning capacity
 * Pain, suffering, and emotional distress
 * Property damage to your vehicle
* **Minimum Insurance Limits:** California's mandatory minimum liability insurance is only $15,000 per person, $30,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $5,000 for property damage. These low amounts are often not enough to cover serious injuries, which makes it crucial to explore all avenues for compensation, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist coverage.

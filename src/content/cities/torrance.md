## What to Do After an Accident in Torrance

The moments after an accident are chaotic and confusing. Taking the right steps can protect your health and your legal rights.

1. **Prioritize Safety:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic to a safe location. Turn on your hazard lights to alert other drivers. Check on yourself and your passengers for injuries.
2. **Contact Emergency Services:** Call 911 immediately. The Torrance Police Department or the California Highway Patrol (for freeway incidents) will respond to the scene. Request paramedics, even if you think your injuries are minor. Adrenaline can mask serious pain.
3. **Seek Medical Attention:** Accept medical treatment at the scene and agree to be transported to a hospital if recommended. Getting evaluated at a major South Bay medical facility or a local clinic creates a crucial record of your injuries. Follow all medical advice and attend all follow-up appointments.
4. **Document the Scene:** If you are physically able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle damage, skid marks, traffic signals, and your injuries.
5. **Gather Information:** Exchange names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details with the other driver(s). Do not apologize or admit fault. Get the names and phone numbers of any witnesses.
6. **File an Official Report:** Cooperate with the responding officer and make sure you get a police report number. This report will be a critical piece of evidence.
7. **Contact a Torrance Personal Injury Lawyer:** Before you speak to an insurance adjuster, contact Reardon Injury Law. The insurance company's goal is to minimize its payout. Our goal is to protect your rights and ensure you receive fair compensation.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system can be intimidating, but understanding a few key California laws is essential for any injury victim.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your final compensation award will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 10% at fault, your recovery will be reduced by 10%.
* **Statute of Limitations:** In most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If you miss this deadline, you will likely lose your right to seek compensation forever.
* **Claims Against Government Entities:** The deadline is much shorter if your claim is against a public entity, such as the City of Torrance, Los Angeles County, or a public transit agency. Under the Government Claims Act, you must file a formal claim within **six months** of the incident.
* **Compensation You Can Recover:** A successful personal injury claim can provide compensation for both economic and non-economic losses, including medical bills (past and future), lost wages, loss of future earning capacity, property damage, and pain and suffering.
* **California's Minimum Insurance Requirements:** The state minimum for liability insurance is only $15,000 per person for injury, $30,000 per accident, and $5,000 for property damage. These low limits are often insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury, which is why it's critical to have an attorney who can identify all potential sources of recovery.

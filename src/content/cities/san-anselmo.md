## What to Do After an Accident in San Anselmo

The steps you take immediately following an accident are crucial for protecting your health and your legal rights.

1.  **Prioritize Safety:** If possible, move your vehicle out of traffic. Turn on your hazard lights. Check on yourself and others involved.
2.  **Call 911:** Report the accident to the police immediately. The San Anselmo Police Department or the Marin County Sheriff's Office will likely respond. A police report is a vital piece of evidence for your claim.
3.  **Seek Medical Attention:** Even if you feel fine, it is essential to get a medical evaluation. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Go to a local urgent care clinic or the emergency room at **Marin General Hospital** in Greenbrae, the primary trauma center for Marin County.
4.  **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, traffic signals, and any visible injuries.
5.  **Exchange Information:** Get the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details from all drivers involved. Also, collect contact information from any witnesses.
6.  **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid discussing who was at fault with anyone at the scene, including the other driver or their insurance company. Stick to the facts of what happened.
7.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you speak with an insurance adjuster, call Reardon Injury Law for a free, no-obligation consultation. We can advise you on how to protect your rights and handle all communications with the insurance companies on your behalf.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system after an injury can be confusing. An experienced personal injury attorney can guide you through California's specific laws to ensure you receive the justice you deserve.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your final award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 10% at fault, your compensation will be reduced by 10%.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** Time is limited for filing a claim. For most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, there is a critical exception: if your claim is against a public entity (e.g., the Town of San Anselmo, Marin County, or a public bus), you must file a formal claim within **six months** of the incident.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's mandatory minimum liability insurance is just $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low amounts are often insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury. We are skilled at identifying all possible sources of compensation, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
*   **Recoverable Damages:** As an injury victim, you may be entitled to compensation for both economic and non-economic losses, including medical bills (past and future), lost wages, loss of future earning capacity, property damage, pain and suffering, and emotional distress.

## What to Do After an Accident in Sunnyvale

The steps you take immediately following an accident are critical for protecting your health and your legal rights. If you are involved in a collision in Sunnyvale, try to follow this guide:

1. **Get to Safety and Call 911:** Your first priority is safety. If possible, move your vehicle out of traffic. Call 911 to report the accident and request emergency medical assistance. The Sunnyvale Department of Public Safety or the California Highway Patrol (for accidents on freeways like US-101) will respond.
2. **Seek Medical Attention:** Accept medical help at the scene and be sure to get a full evaluation at a hospital or urgent care clinic, even if you feel fine. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. A visit to a local facility like **Kaiser Permanente Sunnyvale Medical Center**, or a regional trauma center such as **Santa Clara Valley Medical Center** or **Stanford Health Care** for severe injuries, creates an official record of your condition.
3. **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of everything: vehicle damage, your injuries, skid marks, traffic signals, and the surrounding area.
4. **Exchange Information:** Collect the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details from all other drivers involved. Also, get the names and phone numbers of any witnesses.
5. **Avoid Admitting Fault:** Do not apologize or admit any fault for the accident. Stick to the facts when speaking with police. Do not give a recorded statement to any insurance adjuster until you have spoken with an attorney.
6. **Contact an Attorney:** Before you agree to any settlement, contact a personal injury lawyer. An experienced attorney can help you navigate the claims process, handle communication with insurance companies, and ensure you pursue the full compensation you are entitled to.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires an understanding of California's specific laws. An attorney can guide you, but it is helpful to be aware of these key principles that will affect your case.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially to blame for the accident. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, your final compensation award would be reduced by that 20%. This rule prevents insurance companies from unfairly denying a claim just because you may share a small amount of responsibility.

* **Statute of Limitations:** There are strict deadlines for filing a personal injury lawsuit. In most cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a claim (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, a critical exception applies if your claim is against a government entity—such as the City of Sunnyvale, Santa Clara County, or the State of California. In these cases, you have only **six months** to file a formal administrative claim. Missing this deadline can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.

* **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low amounts are often not enough to cover the costs of a serious injury. An experienced attorney can help identify other potential sources of compensation, such as your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.

* **Available Compensation (Damages):** As a victim, you may be entitled to recover compensation for economic and non-economic losses, including all medical bills (past and future), lost wages, diminished earning capacity, property damage, and compensation for your physical pain and emotional suffering.

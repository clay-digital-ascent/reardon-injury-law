## What to Do After an Accident in Beaumont

The steps you take in the minutes and days following an accident are critical for both your health and your potential legal claim.

1.  **Prioritize Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Check on others involved. Call 911 immediately to report the accident and request emergency medical services. In Beaumont, the **Beaumont Police Department** will likely respond to crashes on city streets, while the **California Highway Patrol (CHP)** handles incidents on I-10 and SR-60.
2.  **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Even if you feel fine, adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Go to an emergency room or an urgent care clinic for a full evaluation. For Beaumont residents, nearby facilities include **San Gorgonio Memorial Hospital** in Banning. For severe, life-threatening injuries, you may be transported to a regional trauma center like **Loma Linda University Medical Center**. A medical record is crucial evidence linking your injuries to the accident.
3.  **Document the Scene:** If it is safe to do so, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, skid marks, traffic signals, and your visible injuries. Get the names and contact information of any witnesses.
4.  **Exchange Information:** Exchange driver's licenses, insurance information, and vehicle registration details with the other driver(s). Do not apologize or admit fault, as these statements can be used against you later.
5.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you speak with an insurance adjuster, contact a personal injury lawyer. The insurance company's goal is to minimize their payout. Your attorney's goal is to protect your rights and maximize your recovery.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system can be confusing, but a basic understanding of California law can empower you. Here are key principles that may apply to your Beaumont injury case:

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your total compensation will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, you can still collect 80% of your damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** For most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If you miss this deadline, you will likely lose your right to seek compensation forever.
*   **Claims Against Government Entities:** The deadline is much shorter if your claim is against a public entity, such as the City of Beaumont or Riverside County (e.g., an accident involving a city vehicle or a dangerous road condition). Under the Government Claims Act, you must file a formal claim within **six months** of the incident.
*   **Compensation You Can Seek:** Victims are entitled to seek compensation for both economic and non-economic losses. This can include medical expenses (past and future), lost wages, loss of future earning capacity, property damage, pain and suffering, and emotional distress.
*   **California's Minimum Insurance:** The state's minimum liability insurance requirements are very low ($15,000 per person/$30,000 per accident for bodily injury). These amounts are often insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury, making it vital to identify all possible sources of recovery.

Your case will be filed and heard at the appropriate courthouse for Riverside County, which for Beaumont residents is typically the **Banning Justice Center**. An experienced attorney can handle all legal filings and procedures on your behalf.

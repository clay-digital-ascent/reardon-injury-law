## What to Do After an Accident in Tulelake

The moments after an accident are confusing and overwhelming. Taking the right steps can protect your health and your legal rights.

1. **Prioritize Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Check on others involved. Call 911 immediately. Depending on your location, this will dispatch either the California Highway Patrol (CHP) for incidents on CA-139 or US-97, or the Siskiyou County Sheriff's Department for accidents on local city streets.
2. **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Even if you feel you are not seriously hurt, it is critical to get a medical evaluation. Adrenaline can mask pain from serious injuries. For emergencies, the nearest full-service hospital is **Sky Lakes Medical Center** in Klamath Falls, Oregon. For less urgent needs, the **Mountain Valleys Health Centers - Tulelake Clinic** can provide an initial assessment.
3. **Document the Scene:** If you are physically able, use your phone to take pictures of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, and any visible injuries. If there were witnesses, ask for their names and contact information.
4. **Exchange Information:** Exchange names, contact details, driver's license numbers, and insurance information with all other drivers involved. Do not discuss fault.
5. **Report the Accident:** Obtain the police report number from the responding officer. This report is a crucial piece of evidence. You can later request a copy from the CHP or the Siskiyou County Sheriff's office.
6. **Contact an Attorney:** Before you speak with an insurance adjuster, contact an experienced personal injury lawyer. An attorney can protect you from lowball settlement offers and handle all communications with the insurance companies, allowing you to focus on your recovery.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system after an injury can be complex. In California, several key laws will impact your claim.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total compensation award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, you can still collect 80% of the damages.
* **Statute of Limitations:** Time is limited for filing a claim. Under California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit. However, a much shorter deadline applies if your claim is against a government entity (e.g., the city, county, or state). The Government Claims Act requires you to file a formal claim within just **six months** of the incident.
* **California's Minimum Insurance Requirements:** The state-mandated minimum liability insurance is only $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. In a serious accident, medical bills can quickly exceed these low limits. An experienced attorney knows how to identify all possible sources of compensation, including underinsured motorist coverage or third-party liability, to ensure you are fully compensated.
* **Available Compensation:** A successful personal injury claim can provide compensation for both economic and non-economic losses. This includes medical expenses (past and future), lost wages, loss of earning capacity, property damage, and compensation for pain, suffering, and emotional distress.

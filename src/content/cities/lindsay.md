## What to Do After an Accident in Lindsay

The moments after an accident are chaotic and stressful, but the steps you take can significantly impact your health and your ability to file a successful injury claim.

1.  **Ensure Your Safety:** If you can, move your vehicle to the side of the road to avoid further collisions. Turn on your hazard lights.
2.  **Call 911 Immediately:** Report the accident to the authorities. Depending on your location, the Lindsay Police Department or the California Highway Patrol (CHP) will respond. This creates an official record and summons medical help.
3.  **Seek Medical Attention:** Even if you feel fine, it is crucial to get a medical evaluation. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Go to a local facility like **Lindsay District Hospital** or, for severe injuries, the trauma center at **Kaweah Health Medical Center** in Visalia. A medical record is vital evidence for your claim.
4.  **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, traffic signals, and any visible injuries.
5.  **Exchange Information:** Get the other driver’s name, contact information, driver’s license number, license plate number, and insurance policy details. Also, collect contact information from any witnesses.
6.  **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid saying things like "I'm sorry" or "It was my fault." Stick to the facts when speaking with police and the other driver.
7.  **Contact an Experienced Injury Attorney:** Before you give a recorded statement to an insurance adjuster, speak with a lawyer. An attorney can protect your rights and handle all communications with the insurance company on your behalf.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system can be intimidating, but understanding your rights is the first step toward securing justice. In California, several key laws will affect your personal injury claim.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially responsible for the accident. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, your total compensation award will simply be reduced by 20%. You do not lose your right to recover entirely.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** Time is critical. Under California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1, you generally have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit. However, there is a major exception: if your claim is against a government entity (such as the City of Lindsay, Tulare County, or a public school), you must file a formal claim within just **six months** of the incident under the Government Claims Act. Missing these deadlines can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of only $15,000 per person, $30,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $5,000 for property damage. In a serious accident, these low amounts are often not enough to cover all your losses. An experienced attorney can help identify all possible sources of recovery, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
*   **Available Compensation (Damages):** As an injury victim, you may be entitled to recover compensation for both economic and non-economic losses. This can include medical expenses (past and future), lost wages, loss of future earning capacity, property damage, pain and suffering, emotional distress, and loss of enjoyment of life. Our goal is to build a case that accounts for the full scope of your damages.

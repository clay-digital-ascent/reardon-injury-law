## What to Do After an Accident in San Juan Capistrano

The actions you take in the minutes and days following an accident are critical for protecting your health and your legal rights. Follow these steps:

1. **Prioritize Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Check on others involved and call 911 immediately. This will dispatch law enforcement (typically the Orange County Sheriff's Department or the California Highway Patrol for freeways) and emergency medical services.
2. **Seek Medical Attention:** Accept paramedic assistance at the scene. Even if you feel you are not seriously hurt, it is vital to be evaluated at an emergency room or urgent care center as soon as possible. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. For severe trauma, you will likely be taken to a nearby trauma center like **Mission Hospital Mission Viejo**. Other excellent facilities like **Hoag Hospital Irvine** are also accessible. This creates an official medical record linking your injuries to the accident.
3. **Document Everything:** Use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle positions, property damage, skid marks, road conditions, and your visible injuries.
4. **Exchange Information:** Obtain the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details from all drivers involved. Also, get the names and phone numbers of any witnesses.
5. **Do Not Admit Fault:** Be polite, but do not apologize or say the accident was your fault. Stick to the facts when speaking with law enforcement. Avoid giving a recorded statement to the other party’s insurance company before consulting with an attorney.
6. **Contact an Attorney:** Before you agree to any settlement, call an experienced personal injury lawyer. A free consultation can provide crucial guidance on how to protect your rights and pursue the full compensation you are owed.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires an understanding of specific California laws that will shape your case.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total recovery will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 10% responsible, you can still collect 90% of your damages.
* **Statute of Limitations:** Time is limited. In most cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, a critical exception applies to claims against government entities (e.g., a city bus, a dangerous public road). For these cases, you must file a formal claim within just **six months** under the Government Claims Act. Missing this deadline can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
* **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's mandatory minimum liability insurance is only $15,000 per person, $30,000 per accident, and $5,000 for property damage. These low amounts are often completely inadequate for covering the costs of a serious injury. An experienced attorney can help identify all potential sources of recovery, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
* **Available Compensation (Damages):** An injury claim allows you to seek compensation for all your losses, which are categorized as economic and non-economic damages. This can include medical bills (past and future), lost wages, diminished earning capacity, property damage, pain and suffering, emotional distress, and loss of enjoyment of life.

## What to Do After an Accident in Chico

The steps you take immediately following an accident are critical for protecting both your health and your legal rights.

1.  **Prioritize Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move to a safe location away from traffic. Call 911 to report the accident and request medical assistance. The Chico Police Department or, for incidents on the highway, the California Highway Patrol (CHP) will respond to create an official report.
2.  **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Accept paramedic assistance and go to an emergency room, such as the one at **Enloe Medical Center** or **Adventist Health Feather River**. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries, and a medical record created right after the accident is vital evidence for your claim.
3.  **Document the Scene:** If you are physically able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene. Capture vehicle damage, license plates, skid marks, road conditions, and any visible injuries. Get the names and contact information of any witnesses.
4.  **Exchange Information:** Exchange driver's license, insurance, and contact information with the other driver(s) involved. However, do not apologize or admit fault. Stick to the facts.
5.  **Contact an Experienced Attorney:** Before you give a recorded statement to any insurance adjuster, contact a personal injury lawyer. An attorney can advise you on your rights and handle all communications with the insurance companies, protecting you from tactics designed to devalue your claim.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires an understanding of California's specific laws. An experienced attorney will manage these legal complexities for you, but knowing your basic rights is empowering.

**Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. For example, if you were found to be 20% at fault, your final compensation award would simply be reduced by 20%. You are not barred from recovery for being partially responsible.

**Statute of Limitations:** Time limits are strict. For most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, a critical exception exists for claims against government entities (e.g., a city-owned vehicle, a dangerous public road). Under the Government Claims Act, you must file a formal claim with the public entity within just **six months** of the injury. Missing this deadline can permanently bar you from seeking compensation.

**Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These amounts are often tragically insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury. This is why it is so important to explore all avenues of recovery, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist coverage.

**Types of Compensation:** Victims of negligence in Chico can seek compensation (known as "damages") for a wide range of losses, including:
*   Past and future medical bills
*   Lost wages and income
*   Loss of future earning capacity
*   Pain, suffering, and emotional distress
*   Property damage

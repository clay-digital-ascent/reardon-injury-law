## Accident Hotspots in Huron

While Huron is a small city with relatively low traffic volume, its position as an agricultural hub creates specific risks on local roadways. Residents should exercise particular caution in the following areas:

* **State Route 198 (SR-198) through Huron:** This is the main artery for the region's agricultural industry. The constant presence of large commercial trucks hauling produce creates significant hazards. These heavy vehicles have large blind spots, require much longer stopping distances, and can cause catastrophic damage in a collision. The rural nature of SR-198 also means drivers must contend with seasonal fog, dust from fields that can reduce visibility, and the risks associated with slow-moving farm equipment entering the roadway. Accidents here often involve complex <a href="/truck-accidents">truck accidents</a> that require specialized legal knowledge.
* **Avenue 12 at SR-198:** As a primary entrance and exit for Huron, this intersection sees a mix of local traffic, commuters heading to Coalinga, and agricultural vehicles. Merging can be dangerous, especially when passenger cars must navigate around large tractors or trucks.

## What to Do After an Accident in Huron

The steps you take immediately following an accident are crucial for protecting both your health and your legal rights.

1. **Get to Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Call 911 immediately to report the accident and request emergency medical services. The Huron Police Department or the Fresno County Sheriff's Office will likely respond.
2. **Seek Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Even if you feel fine, it's essential to get a medical evaluation. Some serious injuries, like internal bleeding or whiplash, may not have immediate symptoms. For severe or life-threatening injuries, you will likely be transported to **Community Regional Medical Center** in Fresno, a Level 1 trauma center. For less critical injuries, the nearest facility is **Coalinga Regional Medical Center**. A medical record is vital evidence for your injury claim.
3. **Document the Scene:** If you are physically able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle damage, skid marks, road conditions, and your visible injuries.
4. **Gather Information:** Exchange contact and insurance information with the other driver(s). Get the names and phone numbers of any witnesses. Do not argue or place blame.
5. **Avoid Admitting Fault:** Do not apologize or say the accident was your fault. Stick to the facts when speaking with law enforcement. Do not give a recorded statement to the other driver's insurance company before consulting with an attorney.
6. **Contact a Personal Injury Lawyer:** Before you speak with insurance adjusters, contact our firm. We can immediately take over communication with the insurance companies, begin our own investigation, and ensure crucial evidence is preserved.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system after an injury can be confusing. It is important to understand the California laws that will impact your case.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. For example, if you are found to be 20% responsible, your total compensation award would be reduced by 20%, but you could still recover the remaining 80%.
* **Statute of Limitations:** In most personal injury cases, California law gives you **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, a critical exception applies to claims against government entities (e.g., a city bus, a public school, or an accident caused by a poorly maintained road). In these cases, you must file a formal claim with the government agency within just **six months** of the incident. Missing these deadlines can permanently bar you from seeking compensation.
* **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's minimum liability insurance requirements are just $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low limits are often not enough to cover the costs of a serious injury. Our attorneys are skilled at identifying all possible sources of compensation, including underinsured motorist coverage and third-party liability.
* **Available Compensation (Damages):** As an injury victim, you may be entitled to recover compensation for both economic and non-economic losses. This can include medical bills (past and future), lost wages, diminished earning capacity, property damage, pain and suffering, emotional distress, and loss of enjoyment of life.

## What to Do After an Accident in Chowchilla

The actions you take in the minutes and hours following an accident are critical for protecting your health and your legal rights. If you are involved in a crash in Chowchilla, follow these steps:

1.  **Ensure Safety and Call 911:** Move to a safe location if possible. Call 911 immediately to report the accident and request emergency medical services. For crashes on city streets, the Chowchilla Police Department will likely respond. For incidents on SR-99, the California Highway Patrol (CHP) will have jurisdiction.
2.  **Seek Medical Attention:** Accept medical help at the scene. Even if you feel fine, it's crucial to get a professional medical evaluation. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries like concussions or internal bleeding. For significant injuries, you may be transported to a nearby facility like **Madera Community Hospital** or a larger trauma center in Fresno.
3.  **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle positions, property damage, skid marks, traffic signals, and your injuries.
4.  **Exchange Information:** Get the other driver's name, contact information, driver's license number, license plate number, and insurance details. Also, collect contact information from any witnesses. Do not admit fault or apologize for the accident.
5.  **File an Official Report:** The responding law enforcement agency will create an accident report. This report is a vital piece of evidence. Make sure you get the report number and know how to obtain a copy later.
6.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you speak with an insurance adjuster, contact an experienced personal injury attorney. An adjuster’s goal is to minimize the insurance company's payout. Your attorney's goal is to protect your interests and maximize your recovery.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system can be confusing, but understanding your basic rights is the first step toward justice. California has specific laws that govern personal injury claims.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California operates under a "pure comparative negligence" system. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, your total compensation award will simply be reduced by 20%. You are not barred from recovery.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** There are strict deadlines for filing a lawsuit. For most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a claim (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, a critical exception exists for claims against government entities (e.g., the City of Chowchilla, Madera County, or the State of California). In these cases, you must file a formal administrative claim within just **six months** of the incident.

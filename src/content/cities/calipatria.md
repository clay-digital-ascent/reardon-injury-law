## What to Do After an Accident in Calipatria

The actions you take in the minutes, hours, and days following an accident are critical for protecting your health and your legal rights. If you are involved in a crash in Calipatria, follow these steps:

1.  **Ensure Your Safety:** If you can, move your vehicle to the side of the road to avoid further collisions. Turn on your hazard lights.
2.  **Call 911:** Report the accident to the police immediately. The Calipatria Police Department will likely respond to incidents within city limits, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles crashes on state routes like SR-111 and SR-115. An official police report is a vital piece of evidence.
3.  **Seek Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Because there are no hospitals in Calipatria, you will need to travel for care. The nearest major facilities are in El Centro, including **El Centro Regional Medical Center** and **Pioneers Memorial Healthcare District**. Go to the emergency room or an urgent care clinic even if you feel fine, as some serious injuries may not show symptoms right away.
4.  **Document the Scene:** If you are physically able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle positions, property damage, skid marks, road conditions, and any visible injuries.
5.  **Exchange Information:** Get the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details from all other drivers involved. Also, get contact information for any witnesses.
6.  **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid saying things like "I'm sorry" or "It was my fault." Stick to the facts when speaking with the police. Do not give a recorded statement to the other driver's insurance company without first consulting an attorney.
7.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you accept any settlement offer, call Reardon Injury Law. An insurance adjuster's primary goal is to minimize their company's payout. We will protect your rights and handle all communications with the insurance companies on your behalf.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires a clear understanding of California's specific laws. Our team will guide you through every step, ensuring you know your rights.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. For example, if you were found to be 20% at fault, your total compensation award would simply be reduced by 20%.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** In most cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If you miss this deadline, you will likely lose your right to seek compensation forever.
*   **Claims Against Government Entities:** The deadline is much shorter if your injury was caused by a public entity, such as poorly maintained roads owned by the city or state. Under the California Government Claims Act, you must file a formal claim within **six months** of the injury. This is a strict and unforgiving deadline.
*   **Compensation You Can Recover:** A successful personal injury claim can provide compensation for both economic and non-economic losses, including:
    *   Past and future medical expenses
    *   Lost wages and loss of future earning capacity
    *   Property damage
    *   Pain and suffering
    *   Emotional distress
*   **California's Minimum Insurance:** The state's minimum liability insurance requirement is only $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. In a serious accident, these amounts are often not enough to cover all your losses, making it crucial to explore all avenues for compensation, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist coverage.

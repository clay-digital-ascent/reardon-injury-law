## What to Do After an Accident in Lawndale

The steps you take immediately following an accident are crucial for protecting both your health and your legal rights.

1.  **Ensure Safety and Contact Authorities:** If you are able, move to a safe location away from traffic. Call 911 immediately. The Los Angeles County Sheriff’s Department (Lennox Station) typically responds to incidents on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles accidents on the I-405 and I-105.
2.  **Seek Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Accept emergency medical services at the scene or go to a nearby hospital like **Harbor-UCLA Medical Center**, **Centinela Hospital Medical Center**, or **Little Company of Mary Hospital**. Some serious injuries, like concussions or internal bleeding, may not have immediate symptoms. A medical record is also vital evidence for your claim.
3.  **Document the Scene:** If you can do so safely, take photos and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle damage, positions of the cars, skid marks, road conditions, and your injuries. Exchange contact and insurance information with the other driver(s) and get the names and phone numbers of any witnesses.
4.  **Avoid Admitting Fault:** Do not apologize or admit fault to anyone at the scene, including the other driver or law enforcement. Stick to the facts of what happened. When you report the accident to your insurance company, provide only the basic information required and decline to give a recorded statement until you have spoken with an attorney.
5.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you accept any settlement offer from an insurance company, consult with a qualified personal injury lawyer. The insurer's goal is to pay as little as possible. An experienced attorney will protect your rights and fight for the full compensation you are owed.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires a clear understanding of California's specific laws. Here are some key principles that will affect your case:

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your final award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 10% responsible, you can still collect 90% of your damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** Time is of the essence. In California, you generally have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, a critical exception exists for claims against government entities (e.g., being hit by a city bus or tripping on a broken public sidewalk). In these cases, you must file a formal claim with the government agency within **six months**.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of only $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for all injuries in an accident, and $5,000 for property damage. These low amounts are often insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury. Our attorneys are skilled at identifying all possible sources of recovery, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
*   **Types of Compensation:** As an injury victim, you may be entitled to recover compensation (known as "damages") for a variety of losses, including past and future medical expenses, lost wages and income, loss of future earning capacity, property damage, and non-economic damages like pain, suffering, and emotional distress.

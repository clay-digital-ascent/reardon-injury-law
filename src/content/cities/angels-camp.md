## What to Do After an Accident in Angels Camp

The moments after an accident are chaotic and confusing. Taking the right steps can protect your health and your legal rights.

1. **Get to Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Call 911 immediately to report the accident and request emergency medical services. For a crash on SR-4 or SR-49, the California Highway Patrol (CHP) will likely respond. For an incident within city limits, it may be the Calaveras County Sheriff's Office.
2. **Seek Immediate Medical Evaluation:** Even if you feel fine, it's critical to get checked by a medical professional. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Go to an emergency room at a facility like **Mark Twain Medical Center** in San Andreas or **Sutter Amador Hospital** in Jackson. For severe, life-threatening injuries, you may be transported to a dedicated trauma center in Stockton or Modesto. A medical record creates a direct link between the accident and your injuries.
3. **Document the Scene:** If you are physically able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of everything: the position of the cars, all vehicle damage, skid marks, road conditions, and your visible injuries.
4. **Exchange Information:** Get the other driver's name, contact information, driver's license number, license plate number, and insurance policy information. Do not apologize or admit any fault.
5. **Do Not Speak to the Insurance Adjuster:** The other party’s insurance company may contact you quickly. Their goal is to get you to accept a low settlement or make a statement that hurts your case. Politely decline to speak with them until you have consulted with an attorney.

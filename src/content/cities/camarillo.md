## What to Do After an Accident in Camarillo

The steps you take immediately following an accident are crucial for protecting your health and your legal rights.

1. **Prioritize Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle to a safe location off the road. Check on others involved and call 911 immediately. The Ventura County Sheriff's Office provides police services to Camarillo and will respond to the scene. Request paramedics if anyone is injured.
2. **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Even if you feel fine, it's vital to get a medical evaluation. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Go to an emergency room at a local hospital like **St. John's Pleasant Valley Hospital** in Camarillo or **Community Memorial Hospital** in nearby Ventura. This creates an official record of your injuries.
3. **Document Everything:** Use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, and any visible injuries. If there are witnesses, get their names and contact information.
4. **Exchange Information:** Get the other driver's name, contact information, driver's license number, license plate number, and insurance policy information. Provide your own information but avoid discussing fault.
5. **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid saying things like "I'm sorry" or "I didn't see you." Stick to the facts when speaking with police and other parties. Any admission of fault can be used against you later.
6. **Contact an Attorney:** Before you speak with an insurance adjuster, contact an experienced personal injury attorney. The insurance company's goal is to minimize their payout. Your attorney's goal is to protect your rights and ensure you are fairly compensated.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires an understanding of specific California laws. Here are some key principles that may affect your case:

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your final compensation will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, your damage award will be reduced by 20%.
* **Statute of Limitations:** In California, you generally have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If your claim is against a government entity (e.g., a city bus, a poorly maintained public road), the deadline is much shorter. You must file a formal claim with the government agency within **six months** of the incident.
* **California's Minimum Insurance:** The state's minimum liability insurance requirements are just $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. In a serious accident, these amounts are often insufficient to cover all damages, making it critical to identify all possible sources of compensation.
* **Available Compensation:** A successful personal injury claim can provide compensation for both economic and non-economic losses. This includes medical expenses (current and future), lost wages, loss of earning capacity, property damage, and non-economic damages like pain, suffering, and emotional distress.

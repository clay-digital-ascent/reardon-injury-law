## What to Do After an Accident in La Canada Flintridge

The moments after an accident are chaotic and stressful. Knowing what to do can protect your health and your legal rights.

1. **Ensure Safety and Call 911:** If you can, move your vehicle out of traffic. Check on others involved. Call 911 immediately to report the accident and any injuries. The Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department (Crescenta Valley Station) is the primary law enforcement agency that will respond to incidents in La Canada Flintridge.
2. **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Even if you feel fine, adrenaline can mask serious injuries. It is crucial to be evaluated by a medical professional. Emergency services may take you to a nearby facility like **USC Verdugo Hills Hospital** in Glendale or the regional trauma center at **Huntington Hospital** in Pasadena. A documented medical visit creates a vital link between the accident and your injuries.
3. **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures of the vehicles, their positions, skid marks, road debris, and any relevant traffic signs or signals. Get the names and contact information of any witnesses.
4. **Exchange Information:** Exchange names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance policy details with the other driver(s). Do not apologize or admit fault.
5. **Report the Accident:** You will need a copy of the official traffic collision report. You can typically request this from the LASD Crescenta Valley Station a week or two after the incident.
6. **Contact an Attorney:** Before you speak to an insurance adjuster, contact an experienced personal injury attorney. The insurance company's goal is to minimize their payout. Our goal is to protect your interests and ensure you are compensated fairly.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires an understanding of key California laws that will directly impact your case.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your total award will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, your compensation will be reduced by 20%.
* **Statute of Limitations:** In most cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If you miss this deadline, you will likely lose your right to sue forever.
* **Government Claims Act:** The deadline is much shorter if your claim is against a government entity—such as the City of La Canada Flintridge, Los Angeles County, or the State of California. In these situations, you must file a formal administrative claim within **six months** of the injury.
* **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's minimum liability insurance requirement is just $15,000 per person, $30,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $5,000 for property damage. These low amounts are often insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury. A skilled attorney can help identify all potential sources of recovery, including your own Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
* **Available Compensation:** Victims of negligence are entitled to seek compensation for both economic and non-economic damages. This can include medical bills (past and future), lost wages, loss of earning capacity, property damage, pain and suffering, and emotional distress.

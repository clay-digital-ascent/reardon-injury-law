## What to Do After an Accident in Pasadena

The steps you take immediately following an accident are crucial for both your health and your potential legal claim.

1.  **Prioritize Safety:** Move to a safe location if you can. Check on others involved and turn on your hazard lights.
2.  **Call 911:** Report the accident to the authorities. Depending on your location, the **Pasadena Police Department** (for city streets) or the **California Highway Patrol (CHP)** (for freeways like the I-210 or SR-110) will respond. A police report is a vital piece of evidence.
3.  **Seek Medical Attention:** Even if you feel fine, it's essential to get a medical evaluation. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Go to an emergency room or an urgent care clinic. In Pasadena, excellent options include **Huntington Health (a Cedars-Sinai affiliate)**, which is a designated Level II Trauma Center, or the nearby **USC Arcadia Hospital**.
4.  **Document Everything:** Take photos and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, your injuries, and any relevant road signs or conditions. Get the names and contact information of any witnesses.
5.  **Do Not Admit Fault:** Be polite, but do not apologize or accept blame. Stick to the facts when speaking with police.
6.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you speak to any insurance adjuster—even your own—contact a personal injury lawyer. The insurance company's goal is to minimize their payout. Your attorney's goal is to protect your rights and maximize your recovery.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system after an injury can be confusing. It’s important to understand the basic laws that govern personal injury claims in California.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total compensation award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, you can still collect 80% of the damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** In most cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If you miss this deadline, you will likely lose your right to sue forever.
*   **Government Claims:** A crucial exception exists for claims against government entities (e.g., being hit by a city bus, or tripping on a broken public sidewalk). Under the California Government Claims Act, you must file a formal claim with the correct government agency within **six months** of the incident. This is a very strict deadline.
*   **California's Minimum Insurance:** The state requires drivers to carry a minimum of only $15,000 per person for bodily injury, $30,000 per accident, and $5,000 for property damage. These low limits are often insufficient to cover serious injuries. An experienced attorney can help identify all potential sources of recovery, including your own uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage.

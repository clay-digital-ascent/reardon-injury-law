## What to Do After an Accident in Cudahy

The steps you take immediately following an accident are crucial for protecting both your health and your legal rights. If you are involved in a collision in Cudahy, follow this guide:

1.  **Ensure Safety and Call 911:** If you can, move your vehicle out of traffic. Check on yourself and others. Immediately call 911 to report the accident and request police and medical assistance. The Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department will likely respond.
2.  **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Even if you feel fine, you may have hidden injuries like whiplash or internal bleeding. Get evaluated by paramedics on the scene or go to a nearby hospital, such as **Huntington Park Hospital** or **PIH Health Hospital-Whittier**. This creates a medical record linking your injuries to the accident.
3.  **Document Everything:** Use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, traffic signals, and any visible injuries. Get the names and contact information of the other driver(s) and any witnesses.
4.  **Exchange Information:** Exchange driver's licenses, insurance policies, and vehicle registration information with the other party. Do not apologize or admit fault to anyone.
5.  **Report the Accident:** Once the police report is available, obtain a copy. This document is a critical piece of evidence for your claim.
6.  **Contact a Cudahy Personal Injury Lawyer:** Before you speak with an insurance adjuster, call Reardon Injury Law. Insurance companies aim to minimize their payouts; we aim to maximize your recovery.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires understanding the specific laws that govern your case in California. Here are some key legal principles that may affect your claim in Cudahy:

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California law allows you to recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total compensation will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, you can still collect 80% of your damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** For most personal injury claims, you have a strict deadline to file a lawsuit. Under California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1, you must file your claim within **two years** of the date of the injury. If you miss this deadline, you will likely lose your right to seek compensation forever.
*   **Government Claims:** If your injury was caused by a public entity—such as a poorly maintained road owned by the City of Cudahy or an accident with a public bus—the rules are even stricter. The Government Claims Act requires you to file a formal notice of your claim within **six months** of the injury.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's minimum liability insurance requirements are very low ($15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 per accident, and $5,000 for property damage). It is common for these minimums to be insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury. An experienced attorney can help you explore other avenues for recovery, such as your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist coverage.

As a victim, you may be entitled to compensation for medical expenses (past and future), lost wages, loss of future earning capacity, property damage, and pain and suffering. All personal injury cases for Cudahy are filed at the **Los Angeles County Superior Court, Southeast District - Norwalk Courthouse**.

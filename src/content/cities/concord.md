While Concord offers a vibrant community, its roads and highways present significant risks for drivers, motorcyclists, and pedestrians. With a population of over 125,000 and its position as a major East Bay hub, traffic congestion and accidents are a serious concern. In fact, Concord ranks 17th among comparable California cities for its high rate of fatal and injury collisions, with 420 such crashes in a single recent year.

Many of these incidents occur on the major arteries that cut through and around the city:

*   **Interstate 680 (I-680):** As a primary commute corridor, I-680 is notorious for heavy congestion, especially at its interchanges. The combination of high traffic volume, narrow lanes in certain sections, and speeding can lead to devastating multi-vehicle pile-ups and serious <a href="/truck-accidents">truck accidents</a>.

*   **Local Streets and Intersections:** Beyond the freeways, Concord's local roads are not immune to danger. Statistics show a high number of speed-related crashes and nighttime collisions within the city. ## What to Do After an Accident in Concord

The steps you take in the minutes, hours, and days following an accident are crucial for protecting both your health and your legal rights.

1.  **Get to Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Call 911 immediately to report the accident and any injuries. The Concord Police Department will likely respond to accidents on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles incidents on I-680 and SR-4.
2.  **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Even if you feel fine, it is essential to be evaluated by a medical professional. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. In Concord, you may be transported to **John Muir Medical Center**, or in cases of severe trauma, to the nearby **Contra Costa Regional Medical Center** in Martinez. A medical record is vital evidence for your claim.
3.  **Document the Scene:** If you are physically able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, and your injuries. Get the names and contact information of any witnesses.
4.  **Exchange Information:** Exchange driver's license, insurance, and contact information with the other driver(s) involved. Do not argue or apologize. Stick to the facts.
5.  **Report the Accident:** Follow the instructions of the responding officer. Be sure to get a report number. You will need this to obtain a copy of the official traffic collision report later.
6.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you give a recorded statement to any insurance company—even your own—speak with a qualified personal injury lawyer. An attorney can protect you from tactics used by adjusters to minimize or deny your claim.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires a clear understanding of California's specific laws. An experienced attorney can ensure your rights are protected every step of the way.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your final award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, you can still collect 80% of your total damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** There is a strict time limit for filing a personal injury lawsuit. In most cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a claim (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, a critical exception exists for claims against government entities (e.g., a city bus, a dangerous public road). In these cases, you must file a formal claim notice within just **six months** of the incident. Missing this deadline can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry a minimum of $15,000 per person for bodily injury, $30,000 per accident, and $5,000 for property damage. These low limits are often completely inadequate to cover the costs of a serious injury. We are skilled at identifying all potential sources of recovery, including uninsured/underinsured motorist policies and third-party liability.
*   **Recoverable Damages:** As an injury victim, you may be entitled to compensation for economic and non-economic losses, including:
    *   Past and future medical expenses
    *   Lost wages and loss of future earning capacity
    *   Pain, suffering, and emotional distress
    *   Property damage

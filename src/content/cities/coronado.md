## What to Do After an Accident in Coronado

The steps you take immediately after an accident are critical for both your health and your potential legal claim. Stay as calm as possible and follow this guide:

1.  **Ensure Safety and Call 911:** If you can, move your vehicle out of traffic. Check on yourself and others for injuries. Call 911 immediately to report the crash and request medical assistance. The Coronado Police Department typically responds to accidents on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles incidents on State Route 75 and the bridge.
2.  **Seek Medical Evaluation:** Your health is the top priority. Accept paramedic assistance and go to the hospital if recommended. For local care, **Sharp Coronado Hospital** is available. For severe or life-threatening injuries, you will likely be transported to a Level 1 trauma center like **UC San Diego Medical Center** in Hillcrest. Seeing a doctor creates an official record of your injuries, which is vital for your claim.
3.  **Document the Scene:** Use your phone to take photos and videos of everything: vehicle positions, property damage, skid marks, traffic signals, and your injuries. Exchange names, contact information, and insurance details with the other driver(s), but do not discuss fault.
4.  **Obtain the Official Report:** Ask the responding officer for a report number. In the following days, you can request a copy from the Coronado Police Department or the local CHP office. This report is a key piece of evidence.
5.  **Contact an Attorney Before Speaking to Insurers:** The other party's insurance adjuster may contact you quickly. Their goal is to minimize their company's payout. Do not give a recorded statement or sign any documents without first speaking to an experienced personal injury attorney who can protect your rights.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system can be confusing, but a few key California laws will directly impact your personal injury claim.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total compensation will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For instance, if you are found to be 10% responsible, you can still collect 90% of your damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** Time is limited for filing a claim. For most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the incident to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, there is a critical exception: if your claim is against a government entity (like the City of Coronado or the State of California), you must file a formal claim within **six months** under the Government Claims Act. Missing this deadline can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
*   **California's Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage.

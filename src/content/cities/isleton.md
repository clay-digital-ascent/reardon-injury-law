## What to Do After an Accident in Isleton

The moments after an accident are chaotic and stressful. Taking the right steps can protect your health and your legal rights.

1. **Prioritize Safety:** Move to a safe location away from traffic if possible. Check on others involved and turn on your hazard lights.
2. **Call 911:** Report the accident immediately. Depending on your location, the California Highway Patrol (CHP) or the Sacramento County Sheriff's Office will likely respond. A formal police report is a crucial piece of evidence.
3. **Seek Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Even if you feel fine, some serious injuries have delayed symptoms. Get evaluated by paramedics on the scene and follow up with a visit to a doctor or a nearby medical facility in Lodi or a hospital in Sacramento. This creates a medical record linking your injuries to the accident.
4. **Document Everything:** If you are able, take photos and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle damage, skid marks, road conditions, and any visible injuries. Get the names and contact information of any witnesses.
5. **Exchange Information:** Exchange names, contact details, driver's license numbers, and insurance information with the other driver(s). Do not apologize or admit fault.
6. **Contact an Attorney:** Before you speak with any insurance adjusters—even your own—call Reardon Injury Law. Insurance companies aim to minimize payouts. We will handle all communications on your behalf to protect you from lowball settlement offers.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires understanding key California laws. Our team will guide you through every step, but here are some important principles that may affect your case:

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California law allows you to recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total compensation will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found 20% at fault, you can still collect 80% of the awarded damages.
* **Statute of Limitations:** In most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If your claim is against a government body (e.g., a city vehicle, a dangerous public road), the deadline is much shorter—you must file a formal claim within **six months** under the Government Claims Act.
* **California's Minimum Insurance Requirements:** The state minimum for liability insurance is just $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. In a serious accident, these low limits are often insufficient to cover all damages, making it critical to identify all possible sources of recovery.
* **Types of Compensation:** You may be entitled to recover damages for medical expenses (past and future), lost wages, loss of future earning capacity, property damage, and non-economic damages like pain, suffering, and emotional distress.

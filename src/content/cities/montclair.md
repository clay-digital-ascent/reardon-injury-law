## What to Do After an Accident in Montclair

The steps you take immediately following an accident are crucial for protecting your health and your legal rights.

1.  **Prioritize Safety and Get Help:** Move to a safe location if possible. Call 911 immediately. The Montclair Police Department typically responds to accidents on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles incidents on the I-10 freeway.
2.  **Seek Medical Attention:** Even if you feel fine, you must get a medical evaluation. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Go to an emergency room at a local hospital like Montclair Hospital Medical Center, Pomona Valley Hospital Medical Center, or San Antonio Regional Hospital. This creates a medical record linking your injuries to the accident.
3.  **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, and your visible injuries. Get the names and contact information of any witnesses.
4.  **Exchange Information:** Exchange driver's license, insurance, and contact information with the other driver(s). Do not apologize or admit fault. Stick to the facts.
5.  **File an Official Report:** Cooperate with the responding law enforcement officer to ensure a police report is filed. This report is a critical piece of evidence.
6.  **Consult with an Attorney:** Before you give a recorded statement to any insurance company—especially the at-fault party's—contact Reardon Injury Law. The insurance adjuster's goal is to minimize their payout, not to protect your interests.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system after an injury can be confusing. An experienced attorney can protect your rights and ensure you understand the laws that affect your claim. Key California laws include:

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California law allows you to recover compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your final award is simply reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found 10% at fault, you can still collect 90% of your damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** For most personal injury claims, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, there is a critical exception: if your claim is against a government entity (e.g., a city bus, a poorly maintained public road), you must file a formal claim within just **six months** under the Government Claims Act. Missing this deadline can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's minimum liability insurance coverage is only $15,000 per person for bodily injury, $30,000 per accident, and $5,000 for property damage. In a serious accident, these low limits are often exhausted quickly. We are skilled at identifying all potential sources of compensation, including underinsured motorist coverage and third-party liability.
*   **Available Compensation (Damages):** As an injury victim, you may be entitled to recover compensation for economic and non-economic losses, including medical bills (past and future), lost wages, loss of earning capacity, property damage, pain and suffering, and emotional distress.

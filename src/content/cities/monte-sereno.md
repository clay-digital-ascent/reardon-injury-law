## What to Do After an Accident in Monte Sereno

The steps you take immediately following an accident are critical for protecting both your health and your legal right to compensation.

1. **Prioritize Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Check on others involved. Call 911 immediately to report the accident and request medical assistance. Law enforcement in Monte Sereno may be handled by the Santa Clara County Sheriff's Office or a neighboring police department. An official police report is a vital piece of evidence.
2. **Seek Medical Evaluation:** Accept emergency medical care at the scene. Even if you feel you are not seriously hurt, you must see a doctor. Adrenaline can mask pain, and some serious injuries have delayed symptoms. Being evaluated at a nearby hospital like **Good Samaritan Hospital** in San Jose, **El Camino Hospital** in Mountain View, or the **Regional Medical Center of San Jose** creates a medical record that links your injuries to the accident.
3. **Document the Scene:** Use your phone to take pictures and videos of everything. Capture vehicle positions, property damage, skid marks, traffic signs, weather conditions, and your visible injuries.
4. **Exchange Information:** Get the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details from all drivers involved. Also, get contact information for any witnesses.
5. **Be Careful What You Say:** Do not apologize or admit fault to anyone at the scene. These statements can be used against you later by an insurance company. Stick to the facts when speaking with police.
6. **Contact an Attorney:** Before you give a recorded statement to any insurance company—even your own—speak with a qualified personal injury attorney. An attorney can advise you of your rights and protect you from tactics used by adjusters to devalue your claim.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system after an injury can be daunting. In California, several key laws will impact your claim.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. For example, if you were found to be 20% at fault, your total compensation award would simply be reduced by 20%. You are not barred from recovery for being partially responsible.
* **Statute of Limitations:** Time is limited. In California, you generally have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (under California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, a critical exception exists for claims against government entities (like the city, county, or a public transit agency). In these cases, you must file a formal claim within just **six months** of the incident. Missing this deadline can permanently bar you from seeking compensation.

## What to Do After an Accident in Menlo Park

The actions you take in the minutes and hours after an accident are crucial for protecting your health and your legal rights.

1.  **Move to Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Call 911 immediately to report the crash and request police and medical assistance. The Menlo Park Police Department typically responds to incidents on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) may respond to accidents on state routes like El Camino Real.
2.  **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Even if you feel fine, adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Getting evaluated by paramedics or at a hospital creates a medical record linking your injuries to the accident. For severe trauma, you may be transported to a nearby facility like **Stanford Health Care - Stanford Hospital** in Palo Alto or **Sequoia Hospital** in Redwood City.
3.  **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures of the vehicles, the surrounding area, traffic signals, road conditions, and your injuries. If there are witnesses, ask for their names and contact information.
4.  **Exchange Information:** Get the name, address, driver's license number, insurance information, and license plate number from all other drivers involved. Do not argue or admit fault.
5.  **Obtain the Official Report:** The police report is a vital piece of evidence. You can typically request a copy from the responding agency (Menlo Park PD or CHP) a week or two after the incident.
6.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you speak with an insurance adjuster, contact an experienced personal injury lawyer. The insurance company's goal is to minimize their payout. Your attorney's goal is to protect your interests and ensure you are fairly compensated.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires an understanding of key California laws. An attorney can handle these complexities for you, but it is helpful to be aware of the rules that will govern your case.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" system. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your final award will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% responsible, you can still collect 80% of the total damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** This is a critical deadline. In California, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If your claim is against a government entity—such as the City of Menlo Park for a poorly maintained road or the State of California for a dangerous condition on SR-82—the deadline is much shorter. You must file a formal claim with the government agency within **six months** of the injury. Missing these deadlines can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
*   **California's Minimum Insurance Requirements:** The state only requires drivers to carry minimum liability coverage of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. In any serious accident, these amounts are rarely enough to cover medical bills, lost wages, and other losses. Our firm is skilled at identifying all potential sources of recovery, including underinsured motorist coverage and third-party liability.
*   **Types of Compensation:** A successful personal injury claim can provide compensation for both economic and non-economic losses. This can include medical expenses (past and future), lost income, property damage, as well as pain and suffering, emotional distress, and loss of enjoyment of life.

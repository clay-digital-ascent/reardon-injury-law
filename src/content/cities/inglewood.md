## Accident Hotspots in Inglewood

While an accident can happen anywhere, certain areas in Inglewood are known for their high risk due to traffic volume, design, and driver behavior. Understanding these hotspots can highlight the challenges drivers, motorcyclists, and pedestrians face every day.

*   **Century Blvd & Inglewood Ave:** According to crash data, this is one of the most dangerous intersections in the city. It sees a significant number of broadside (T-bone) collisions, often linked to traffic signal timing and right-of-way violations. The risk here is particularly high during evening commute hours.
*   **Century Blvd & La Cienega Blvd:** Proximity to the I-405 and LAX makes this intersection a chaotic mix of local traffic, airport travelers, and commuters. It has a high rate of broadside crashes and is especially dangerous for people on foot, with a significant number of <a href="/pedestrian-accidents">pedestrian accidents</a> caused by unsafe speeds and signal issues.
*   **Crenshaw Blvd & W Century Blvd:** The combination of heavy traffic, speeding, and limited visibility near freeway ramps makes this another high-risk zone. Merging traffic from the freeway often conflicts with local drivers, leading to serious collisions.
*   **Manchester Blvd & La Brea Ave:** As a major east-west artery connecting Inglewood with greater Los Angeles, Manchester Boulevard experiences constant traffic flow. The intersection with La Brea Avenue is a frequent site of injury-causing accidents, from rear-end collisions to dangerous T-bone crashes.
*   **I-405 and I-105 Freeways:** The on-ramps and off-ramps for these major freeways are inherently dangerous. Drivers transitioning from high-speed freeway travel to slower local roads, and vice-versa, create a volatile environment where serious <a href="/car-accidents">car accidents</a> and <a href="/truck-accidents">truck accidents</a> are common.

## What to Do After an Accident in Inglewood

The steps you take immediately following an accident are crucial for protecting your health and your legal rights.

1.  **Get to Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Call 911 to report the accident and request medical assistance. The Inglewood Police Department typically responds to accidents on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles incidents on the I-405 and I-105.
2.  **Seek Immediate Medical Care:** Even if you feel fine, it's essential to get a medical evaluation. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Go to an emergency room at a facility like **Centinela Hospital Medical Center** or your primary doctor. For severe trauma, you may be transported to a regional Level I trauma center like **UCLA Medical Center**.
3.  **Document the Scene:** Use your phone to take photos and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle positions, property damage, skid marks, traffic signals, and your injuries. Exchange contact and insurance information with all other drivers involved.
4.  **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid saying things like "I'm sorry" or "I didn't see you." Stick to the facts when speaking with police. Do not give a recorded statement to the other party’s insurance adjuster without first speaking to an attorney.
5.  **Contact an Inglewood Personal Injury Lawyer:** An experienced attorney can immediately begin protecting your interests, manage communications with insurance companies, and ensure crucial evidence is preserved.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires understanding key California laws that will affect your case.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your final compensation award will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, your recovery will be reduced by 20%.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** There is a strict time limit for filing a personal injury lawsuit. In most cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a claim (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). Critically, if your injury was caused by a public entity—such as a city bus, a government employee, or a dangerous condition on public property—you must file a formal claim with that government agency within just **six months** of the incident.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's mandatory minimum liability insurance is very low: $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. In a serious accident, these amounts are rarely enough to cover medical bills and lost wages, which is why your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage is so important.
*   **Available Compensation (Damages):** As an injury victim, you may be entitled to recover compensation for economic and non-economic losses, including:
    *   Past and future medical expenses
    *   Lost wages and loss of future earning capacity
    *   Property damage
    *   Pain, suffering, and emotional distress
    *   Loss of enjoyment of life

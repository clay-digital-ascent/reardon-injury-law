## Accident Hotspots in Needles

While Needles itself is a small town, it sits at a critical junction of major transportation corridors, exposing residents and visitors to significant risks. The vast majority of serious collisions in the area occur not on quiet local streets, but on the high-speed highways that cut through the desert.

The most prominent risk zone is the **Interstate 40 corridor**. As a primary east-west route connecting California to Arizona and beyond, I-40 is constantly filled with long-haul commercial trucks and travelers driving at high speeds. Driver fatigue, unsafe speed, and the sheer volume of traffic contribute to devastating <a href="/truck-accidents">truck accidents</a> and multi-vehicle <a href="/car-accidents">car accidents</a>. The transition near the state border can be particularly hazardous as drivers adjust to different traffic patterns.

**State Route 95 (SR-95)** also presents dangers. This north-south highway intersects with I-40 and serves as a popular route for tourists heading to the Colorado River, Lake Havasu, and Las Vegas. This seasonal influx of traffic often includes drivers who are unfamiliar with the roads, distracted by navigation, or operating recreational vehicles. These conditions increase the risk for all motorists, especially those enjoying the open road on <a href="/motorcycle-accidents">motorcycle accidents</a>. ## What to Do After an Accident in Needles

The steps you take immediately following an accident are critical for protecting your health and your legal rights.

1. **Prioritize Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic and turn on your hazard lights. Call 911 immediately to report the accident and request medical assistance. For a crash on I-40 or SR-95, the California Highway Patrol (CHP) will likely respond. For an incident within city limits, the Needles Police Department will have jurisdiction.
2. **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Allow paramedics to evaluate you at the scene. It is vital to get a full medical evaluation at a hospital, even if you feel fine. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Go to **Needles District Hospital** for an initial check-up. Be aware that severe, life-threatening injuries may require air or ground transport to the nearest Level II trauma center, **Arrowhead Regional Medical Center** in Colton, which is over 140 miles away. The cost of this transport alone can be substantial.
3. **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of everything: the position of the cars, all vehicle damage, skid marks, road conditions, and any visible injuries you have.
4. **Exchange Information:** Get the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details from all other drivers involved. Also, get the names and phone numbers of any witnesses.
5. **Do Not Admit Fault:** When speaking with other drivers or the police, state only the facts. Avoid saying "I'm sorry" or accepting any blame, as these statements can be used against you later.
6. **Contact an Attorney:** Before you give a recorded statement to any insurance company—even your own—contact Reardon Injury Law. The insurance adjuster's goal is to minimize their company's payout, not to protect your interests. We will handle all communications with the insurance companies on your behalf.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires a clear understanding of California's specific laws. An experienced attorney ensures these laws are used to your advantage.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your final award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 10% at fault, you can still collect 90% of your total damages.
* **Statute of Limitations:** In most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If you miss this deadline, you will likely lose your right to seek compensation forever.
* **Claims Against Government Entities:** A crucial exception exists for claims against public entities (e.g., a city bus, a county vehicle, or an injury from a poorly maintained public road). In these cases, you must file a formal claim with the government agency within **six months** of the incident.
* **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's minimum liability insurance coverage is just $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. For serious injuries, especially those requiring extended care or transport to a distant trauma center, these minimums are woefully inadequate. We are skilled at identifying all possible sources of compensation to cover the true cost of your recovery.
* **Recoverable Damages:** As an injury victim, you may be entitled to compensation for economic and non-economic losses, including medical bills (past and future), lost wages, diminished earning capacity, property damage, and pain and suffering.

## What to Do After an Accident in Buena Park

The moments after an accident are disorienting, but the steps you take can significantly impact your health and your ability to recover fair compensation.

1.  **Ensure Your Safety:** If possible, move your vehicle out of traffic to a safe location. Turn on your hazard lights to alert other drivers.
2.  **Call 911:** Report the accident immediately. The **Buena Park Police Department** will typically respond to incidents on city streets, while the **California Highway Patrol (CHP)** handles crashes on major freeways like I-5 and SR-91. A police report is a crucial piece of evidence.
3.  **Seek Medical Attention:** This is the most important step. Even if you feel fine, adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Go to an emergency room or your doctor as soon as possible. Depending on the severity, paramedics may transport you to a local hospital like **Mission Hospital Regional Medical Center** or a designated trauma center such as **Hoag Hospital Irvine** or **Los Angeles County + USC Medical Center**.
4.  **Document the Scene:** If you are able, take photos and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, and any visible injuries. Get the names and contact information of any witnesses.
5.  **Exchange Information:** Exchange names, contact details, driver's license numbers, and insurance information with the other driver(s). Do not apologize or admit fault.
6.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you speak with any insurance adjusters, contact an experienced personal injury attorney. The insurance company's goal is to minimize their payout. Your attorney's goal is to protect your rights and ensure you receive full and fair compensation for your losses.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system after an injury can be daunting. In California, several key laws will influence your claim.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your final compensation award will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, you can still collect 80% of your total damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** There are strict deadlines for filing a personal injury lawsuit. In most cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a claim (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, the deadline is much shorter if your claim is against a government entity (e.g., the City of Buena Park, Orange County, or the State of California). In these cases, you must file a formal claim within **six months** of the injury. Missing these deadlines can permanently bar you from seeking compensation.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of only $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. In a serious accident, these low amounts are often exhausted quickly. An experienced attorney can help identify all potential sources of recovery, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
*   **Recoverable Damages:** As an injury victim, you may be entitled to compensation for economic and non-economic losses, including past and future medical expenses, lost wages and earning capacity, property damage, and pain and suffering.

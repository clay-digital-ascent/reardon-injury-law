## What to Do After an Accident in Pleasant Hill

The steps you take immediately following an accident are critical for your health and your potential legal claim.

1. **Ensure Safety:** If possible, move your vehicle out of traffic to a safe location. Turn on your hazard lights to alert other drivers.
2. **Call 911:** Report the accident to the Pleasant Hill Police Department or, if on the freeway, the California Highway Patrol (CHP). A police report is a crucial piece of evidence. Request an ambulance if anyone is injured.
3. **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Accept medical help at the scene. Even if you feel fine, go to an emergency room or an urgent care clinic for a full evaluation. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Area hospitals like **John Muir Medical Center** or **Kaiser Permanente Medical Center in Walnut Creek** are equipped to diagnose and treat accident-related trauma.
4. **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, traffic signals, and any visible injuries.
5. **Exchange Information:** Get the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details of all drivers involved. Also, get contact information for any witnesses.
6. **Avoid Admitting Fault:** Do not apologize or say the accident was your fault. Stick to the facts when speaking with police and other drivers. Any admission can be used against you by an insurance company.
7. **Contact an Attorney:** Before you give a recorded statement to an insurance adjuster, contact an experienced personal injury attorney. We can advise you on your rights and handle all communications with the insurance companies on your behalf.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system after an injury can be overwhelming. As a California law firm, we want to ensure you understand the key laws that will affect your Pleasant Hill injury claim.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total compensation will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 10% responsible, you can still collect 90% of your damages.
* **Statute of Limitations:** In California, you have a limited time to file a lawsuit. For most personal injury cases, the deadline is **two years** from the date of the injury (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, there is a critical exception: if your claim is against a government entity (e.g., the City of Pleasant Hill for a poorly maintained road, or a public bus), you must file a formal claim within just **six months** under the Government Claims Act. Missing these deadlines can permanently bar you from seeking compensation.
* **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low amounts are often not enough to cover the costs of a serious injury. An experienced attorney can help you identify other potential sources of recovery, such as your own Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
* **Recoverable Damages:** Victims of negligence are entitled to seek compensation for both economic and non-economic losses. This can include medical expenses (current and future), lost income, property damage, loss of earning capacity, pain and suffering, and emotional distress. Our firm’s medical expertise is crucial for accurately calculating the full extent of these damages.

## What to Do After an Accident in Monterey Park

The steps you take immediately following an accident are crucial for protecting both your health and your legal rights.

1. **Ensure Safety and Contact Authorities:** If possible, move to a safe location away from traffic. Call 911 immediately. The Monterey Park Police Department will likely respond to accidents on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles incidents on freeways like the I-10 and I-710.
2. **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Even if you feel fine, some serious injuries have delayed symptoms. It is vital to get a medical evaluation. Paramedics can transport you to a nearby hospital such as **Garfield Medical Center** in Alhambra, **LAC+USC Medical Center**, or **Huntington Hospital** in Pasadena.
3. **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle damage, license plates, skid marks, and any relevant traffic signs or signals. Get the names and contact information of any witnesses.
4. **Exchange Information:** Exchange driver's licenses, insurance information, and contact details with the other driver(s) involved. Do not apologize or admit fault.
5. **File an Official Report:** Obtain the police report number from the responding officer. This report is a critical piece of evidence for your claim.
6. **Contact an Attorney:** Before you speak with any insurance adjusters—even your own—contact a personal injury lawyer. Insurance companies aim to minimize payouts. An attorney at Reardon Injury Law can protect you from lowball settlement offers and manage all communication on your behalf.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires an understanding of California's specific laws. An experienced attorney can guide you through the process and ensure your rights are protected.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found 20% at fault, you can still collect 80% of the damages.
* **Statute of Limitations:** In California, you have a limited time to file a claim. For most personal injury cases, you must file a lawsuit within **two years** of the date of the injury (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, a much shorter and stricter deadline applies if your claim is against a government entity, such as the City of Monterey Park or Los Angeles County. In these cases, you must file a formal claim within **six months** of the incident.
* **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry minimum liability coverage of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low amounts are often completely inadequate to cover the costs of a serious injury. This is why having an attorney who can identify all possible sources of recovery is so important.
* **Types of Compensation:** A successful personal injury claim can provide compensation (damages) for both economic and non-economic losses. This can include medical bills (past and future), lost wages, loss of earning capacity, property damage, pain and suffering, and emotional distress.

## What to Do After an Accident in Malibu

The moments after an accident are chaotic and confusing. Taking the right steps can protect your health and your legal rights.

1. **Prioritize Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move to a safe location away from traffic. Call 911 immediately to report the crash and request emergency medical services. The Los Angeles County Sheriff’s Department (Lost Hills Station) typically responds to incidents in Malibu.
2. **Seek Medical Attention:** Accept medical help at the scene and get a full evaluation at a hospital. Because Malibu does not have its own hospital, you will likely be transported to a facility in a nearby community like Santa Monica. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries, and a medical record is crucial for your claim.
3. **Document Everything:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, traffic signals, weather conditions, and any visible injuries.
4. **Exchange Information:** Get the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details from all other drivers involved. Also, try to get contact information from any witnesses.
5. **Report the Accident, But Be Careful:** You must report the accident to your insurance company, but provide only the basic facts. Do not give a recorded statement, admit any fault, or accept a quick settlement offer before speaking with an attorney.
6. **Contact a Malibu Personal Injury Lawyer:** An experienced attorney can immediately take over communication with insurance companies, begin an investigation, and ensure you are protected from tactics designed to devalue your claim.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires understanding key California laws that will directly impact your case.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your final compensation award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, your compensation would be reduced by 20%.
* **Statute of Limitations:** Time is critical. In California, you have a **two-year statute of limitations** (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1) to file a personal injury lawsuit. However, a critical exception applies to claims against government entities (e.g., the City of Malibu, Caltrans for a dangerous road condition, or a public bus). In these cases, you must file a formal claim within just **six months** of the injury. Missing this deadline can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
* **California's Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry a minimum of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These amounts are often tragically insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury. This is why it is so important to have an attorney who can explore all possible sources of recovery.
* **Types of Compensation:** A successful personal injury claim can secure compensation for a wide range of damages, including medical bills (past and future), lost wages, loss of future earning capacity, property damage, and non-economic damages like pain, suffering, and emotional distress.

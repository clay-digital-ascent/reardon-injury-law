## What to Do After an Accident in Cathedral City

The actions you take in the minutes and days following an accident are crucial for protecting your health and your legal rights.

1.  **Prioritize Safety and Call 911:** Move to a safe location if possible and immediately call 911. The Cathedral City Police Department will likely respond to accidents on surface streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles incidents on I-10. A police report is vital evidence.
2.  **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Paramedics can assess you at the scene, but you should always get a full evaluation at a hospital or urgent care center, such as one of the medical centers in nearby Palm Springs or Rancho Mirage. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries, and a medical record creates a direct link between the accident and your harm.
3.  **Document Everything:** Use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, and your visible injuries. Get the names and contact information of the other driver(s) and any witnesses.
4.  **Report the Accident, But Be Brief:** Notify your insurance company that an accident occurred, but do not give a recorded statement or admit any fault. Stick to the basic facts. The other driver's insurance adjuster may call you—it is best not to speak with them until you have consulted an attorney.
5.  **Contact a Cathedral City Personal Injury Lawyer:** Before you accept any settlement offer or sign any documents, contact our firm. We can advise you on your rights and help you avoid common mistakes that could jeopardize your claim.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system can be intimidating, but a basic understanding of your rights is empowering. In California, several key laws will affect your personal injury claim.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California law allows you to recover damages even if you were partially responsible for the accident. Your total compensation will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 10% at fault, you can still collect 90% of your damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** You have a limited time to take legal action. For most personal injury cases, you must file a lawsuit within **two years** of the date of the injury (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, if your claim is against a government entity—such as the City of Cathedral City or Riverside County—you have only **six months** to file a formal government claim. Missing these deadlines can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's mandatory minimum liability insurance is only $15,000 per person, $30,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $5,000 for property damage. In a serious accident, these amounts are often not enough to cover all your losses. An experienced attorney can investigate other potential sources of recovery, such as umbrella policies or Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
*   **Recoverable Damages:** As an injury victim, you may be entitled to compensation for both economic and non-economic losses. This can include medical bills (past and future), lost wages, loss of future earning capacity, property damage, and compensation for your physical pain, mental anguish, and loss of enjoyment of life.

Any personal injury case filed for an accident in Cathedral City will be heard at the **Larson Justice Center in Indio**, which is part of the Riverside Superior Court system. Our firm is prepared to litigate your case there if a fair settlement cannot be reached.

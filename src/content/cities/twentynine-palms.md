## What to Do After an Accident in Twentynine Palms

The actions you take in the minutes and days following an accident are crucial for your health and your potential legal claim. Stay as calm as possible and follow these steps:

1.  **Ensure Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle to a safe location off the road. Check on yourself and others involved. Call 911 immediately to report the accident and request medical assistance. The San Bernardino County Sheriff's Department or the California Highway Patrol (CHP) will likely respond.
2.  **Seek Medical Attention:** Adrenaline can mask the severity of injuries. It is vital to be evaluated by a medical professional, even if you feel fine. Go to **Twentynine Palms Hospital** for an initial assessment. For severe trauma, you may be transported to a larger trauma center in San Bernardino County. A medical record is critical evidence for your claim.
3.  **Document the Scene:** Use your phone to take photos and videos of everything: vehicle positions, property damage, skid marks, traffic signs, and any visible injuries.
4.  **Exchange Information:** Get the other driver's name, contact information, driver's license number, license plate number, and insurance policy information. Do not discuss fault or apologize; simply gather the facts.
5.  **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid saying things like "I'm sorry" or "I didn't see you," as these statements can be used against you by insurance companies. Provide a factual statement to the responding law enforcement officers.
6.  **Contact an Experienced Attorney:** Before you give a recorded statement to any insurance adjuster, contact Reardon Injury Law. The insurance company's goal is to minimize their payout. Our goal is to protect your rights and ensure you are treated fairly.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system after an injury can be overwhelming. As your legal team, we will handle all the complexities for you. Here are some key California laws that may affect your case:

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California law allows you to recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, your final compensation award will be reduced by 20%. This rule ensures that you are not unfairly barred from recovery simply for contributing to the incident in a minor way.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** In most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, a critical exception applies to claims against government entities (e.g., a city bus, a poorly maintained public road). In these cases, you must file a formal claim within **six months** of the incident under the Government Claims Act. Missing these deadlines can permanently bar you from seeking compensation.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's minimum liability insurance is only $15,000 per person for bodily injury, $30,000 per accident, and $5,000 for property damage. These low limits are often exhausted quickly in a serious accident. Our firm excels at identifying all possible sources of recovery, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
*   **Available Compensation:** A successful personal injury claim can help you recover costs for medical bills (past and future), lost wages, diminished earning capacity, property damage, and non-economic damages like pain, suffering, and emotional distress.

All civil personal injury lawsuits arising from accidents in Twentynine Palms are filed and heard at the **San Bernardino County Superior Court - Joshua Tree District Courthouse**. Our firm is prepared to represent you at every stage of the legal process.

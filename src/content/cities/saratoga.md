## What to Do After an Accident in Saratoga

The actions you take in the minutes and days following an accident are critical for protecting your health and your legal rights. If you are involved in a collision in or around Saratoga, follow these steps:

1.  **Ensure Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle to a safe location off the roadway. Check on others involved and call 911 immediately. Report the accident and request an ambulance for anyone who is injured. Depending on your location, the Santa Clara County Sheriff's Office or the California Highway Patrol (CHP) will likely respond.
2.  **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Even if you feel fine, it is essential to be evaluated by a medical professional. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Go to a nearby hospital like **El Camino Hospital** in Mountain View, **Good Samaritan Hospital** in San Jose, or, for severe trauma, the **Regional Medical Center of San Jose**. A documented medical visit creates a crucial link between the accident and your injuries.
3.  **Document the Scene:** Use your phone to take extensive photos and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle damage, skid marks, road conditions, and any visible injuries. If there are witnesses, get their names and contact information.
4.  **Exchange Information:** Exchange driver's license, insurance, and contact information with the other driver(s). Do not apologize or admit fault, as these statements can be used against you later.
5.  **Do Not Speak to Insurance Adjusters:** The other party's insurance adjuster may contact you quickly. Their goal is to minimize their company's payout. Politely decline to give a recorded statement until you have spoken with a personal injury attorney.
6.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you agree to any settlement, consult with an experienced personal injury lawyer. We can help you understand your rights, investigate the accident, and handle all communications with the insurance companies on your behalf.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system after an injury can be daunting. In California, several key laws will impact your personal injury claim.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your total compensation will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 10% responsible for a crash, you can still collect 90% of your total damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** In most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, there is a critical exception: if your claim is against a government entity (like the city of Saratoga, Santa Clara County, or the state), you must file a formal claim notice within **six months** of the accident under the Government Claims Act. Missing these deadlines can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low limits are often completely inadequate to cover the costs of a serious injury. An experienced attorney can help identify all potential sources of recovery, including underinsured motorist coverage or third-party liability.
*   **Recoverable Damages:** A successful personal injury claim can provide compensation for economic and non-economic losses, including medical bills (past and future), lost wages and earning capacity, property damage, and pain, suffering, and emotional distress.

## What to Do After an Accident in Signal Hill

The actions you take immediately following an accident are critical for protecting both your health and your legal rights. Follow these steps:

1.  **Ensure Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Check on others involved and call 911 immediately to report the crash and request emergency medical services. The Signal Hill Police Department or, if on a freeway, the California Highway Patrol (CHP) will likely respond.
2.  **Seek Medical Attention:** Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Even if you feel fine, it is essential to be evaluated by a medical professional. Paramedics can assess you at the scene, but you should also follow up at an emergency room or urgent care center. Major nearby hospitals include **Long Beach Medical Center** and **MemorialCare Miller Children's & Women's Hospital Long Beach**. In cases of severe trauma, you may be transported to **Los Angeles County + USC Medical Center**.
3.  **Document the Scene:** Use your phone to take pictures and videos of everything: vehicle positions, property damage, skid marks, traffic signals, and your injuries. Exchange contact and insurance information with the other driver(s), but do not discuss fault.
4.  **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid saying things like "I'm sorry" or "I didn't see you." These statements can be interpreted as an admission of guilt and used against you by insurance companies. Stick to the facts when speaking with law enforcement.
5.  **Contact an Attorney Before Speaking to Insurers:** The other party's insurance adjuster may contact you quickly, seeking a recorded statement. Their goal is to minimize their company's payout. Before you speak to them, contact an experienced personal injury attorney to learn your rights and protect your claim.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires an understanding of California's specific laws. Here are some key concepts that may affect your case:

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California law allows you to recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your final compensation award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, you can still collect 80% of the total damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** In most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, there is a critical exception: if your claim is against a government entity (like the city, county, or a public transit agency), you must file a formal administrative claim within **six months** of the incident under the Government Claims Act. Missing these deadlines can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of only $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low limits are often insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury, making it vital to identify all potential sources of recovery, including your own Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
*   **Available Compensation (Damages):** A successful personal injury claim can provide compensation for economic and non-economic losses, including past and future medical expenses, lost wages, diminished earning capacity, property damage, and pain and suffering.

## What to Do After an Accident in Woodlake

The moments after an accident are chaotic and stressful. Knowing what to do can protect your health and your legal rights. If you are involved in an accident in Woodlake, follow these steps:

1. **Ensure Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle to a safe location off the road. Check on others involved. Call 911 immediately to report the accident and any injuries. The Woodlake Police Department or Tulare County Sheriff's Office will likely respond.
2. **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Even if you feel fine, some serious injuries have delayed symptoms. Allow paramedics to evaluate you at the scene and get a full medical check-up at a hospital like **Kaweah Health Medical Center** in Visalia or **Adventist Health Tulare**.
3. **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of everything. Capture vehicle damage, skid marks, road conditions, traffic signals, and your injuries.
4. **Exchange Information:** Get the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details from all drivers involved. Also, get contact information for any witnesses. Do not discuss fault.
5. **Report the Crash:** The responding law enforcement agency will create an official report. You will need this for your insurance claim. Be sure to ask the officer for the report number.
6. **Contact a Personal Injury Attorney:** Before you speak to an insurance adjuster, contact an experienced attorney. The insurance company's goal is to pay as little as possible. An attorney will protect your interests and handle all communications on your behalf.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires an understanding of California's specific laws. An experienced attorney can guide you through the complexities, but here are some key principles that may affect your case.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your total compensation will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are awarded $100,000 but found to be 20% at fault, you would receive $80,000.
* **Statute of Limitations:** In California, you generally have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If you miss this deadline, you will likely lose your right to seek compensation forever.
* **Government Claims Act:** A critical exception to the two-year rule applies if your claim is against a government entity—such as the City of Woodlake, Tulare County, or the State of California. In these cases, you must file a formal claim with the correct government agency within **six months** of the injury.
* **Types of Compensation:** Victims of negligence are entitled to seek compensation for both economic and non-economic losses. This can include medical bills (past and future), lost wages, loss of future earning capacity, property damage, pain and suffering, emotional distress, and loss of enjoyment of life.

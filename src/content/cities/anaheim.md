## What to Do After an Accident in Anaheim

The moments after an accident are chaotic and confusing. Taking the right steps can protect your health and your legal rights.

1.  **Prioritize Safety:** If you can, move your vehicle out of traffic to a safe location. Turn on your hazard lights. Check on yourself and your passengers.
2.  **Call 911:** Report the accident immediately. The **Anaheim Police Department** typically responds to incidents on city streets, while the **California Highway Patrol (CHP)** handles collisions on the I-5, SR-91, and SR-57. A police report is a critical piece of evidence.
3.  **Seek Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Accept paramedic assistance or go to an emergency room. For severe, life-threatening injuries, **UCI Medical Center** in the neighboring city of Orange is the official Level I Trauma Center for the region. For other urgent needs, **Anaheim Global Medical Center** is a local option. Even if you feel fine, seeing a doctor is crucial, as many serious injuries have delayed symptoms.
4.  **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures of the vehicles, damage, license plates, skid marks, traffic signals, and your injuries. Get the names and contact information of any witnesses.
5.  **Exchange Information:** Exchange driver's license, insurance, and contact information with the other driver(s). Do not apologize or admit fault.
6.  **Contact a Lawyer:** Before you speak to an insurance adjuster, contact an experienced Anaheim personal injury attorney. The insurance company's goal is to minimize their payout; our goal is to maximize your recovery.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system can be intimidating, but understanding a few key California laws is essential for protecting your rights after an injury.

*   **Statute of Limitations:** In California, you generally have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If you miss this deadline, you will likely lose your right to seek compensation forever.
*   **Claims Against Government Entities:** The deadline is much shorter if your claim is against a public entity, such as an OCTA bus, a City of Anaheim vehicle, or due to a dangerously maintained public road. You must file a formal notice of claim within **six months** of the injury.
*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your total compensation will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found 20% at fault, you can still collect 80% of your total damages.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's mandatory minimum liability insurance is just $15,000 per person, $30,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $5,000 for property damage. These low limits are often insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury, which is why having your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UM/UIM) coverage is so important.

All personal injury cases for incidents occurring in Anaheim are filed at the **North Justice Center in Fullerton**, which is part of the Superior Court of California for the County of Orange. Our firm is intimately familiar with the procedures and personnel at this courthouse.

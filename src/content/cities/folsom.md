## What to Do After an Accident in Folsom

The moments following an accident are chaotic and stressful. Knowing what to do can protect your health and your legal rights. If you are involved in a crash in Folsom, follow these steps:

1.  **Prioritize Safety:** Move to a safe location away from traffic if possible. Check on others involved and turn on your vehicle's hazard lights.
2.  **Call 911:** Report the accident immediately. The Folsom Police Department will likely respond to incidents on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles crashes on US-50. Request medical assistance for anyone who is injured.
3.  **Seek Medical Attention:** Even if you feel fine, it's crucial to get a medical evaluation. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Go to an urgent care clinic or the emergency room at a facility like **Mercy Hospital of Folsom**. For severe, life-threatening injuries, you may be transported to the trauma center at **UC Davis Medical Center** in Sacramento. A medical record is vital evidence for your claim.
4.  **Document Everything:** If you are able, take photos and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle damage, positions of the cars, skid marks, road conditions, and any visible injuries. Get the names, contact information, and insurance details of all drivers involved, as well as contact information for any witnesses.
5.  **Do Not Admit Fault:** Be polite, but do not apologize or accept blame for the accident. Stick to the facts when speaking with police. Any admission of fault can be used against you by insurance companies.
6.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you speak with any insurance adjusters, call an experienced personal injury lawyer. An attorney can handle all communications on your behalf, protect you from lowball settlement offers, and ensure your rights are preserved.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires an understanding of key California laws that will directly impact your case. An experienced attorney can guide you through these complexities, but here are some fundamental principles:

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your total compensation award will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, your final award will be reduced by 20%.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** This is a critical deadline. In California, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If you miss this deadline, you will likely lose your right to sue forever.
*   **Claims Against Government Entities:** The deadline is much shorter if your claim is against a public entity, such as the City of Folsom, Sacramento County, or the State of California (for example, due to a poorly maintained road). Under the California Government Claims Act, you must file a formal claim with the correct government agency within **six months** of the injury.
*   **California's Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry a minimum of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. In any serious accident, these low amounts are often insufficient to cover all damages. We can help you explore all possible sources of recovery, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
*   **Types of Compensation:** You may be entitled to recover compensation for both economic and non-economic damages. Economic damages include tangible losses like medical bills, future medical care, lost wages, and loss of future earning capacity. Non-economic damages compensate you for intangible losses, such as pain, suffering, emotional distress, and loss of enjoyment of life.

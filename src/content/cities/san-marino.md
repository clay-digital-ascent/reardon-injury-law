## What to Do After an Accident in San Marino

The actions you take immediately following an accident are critical for protecting your health and your legal rights. If you are involved in a collision in or around San Marino, follow these steps:

1. **Ensure Your Safety:** If possible, move your vehicle out of traffic to a safe location. Turn on your hazard lights to alert other drivers.
2. **Contact Emergency Services:** Call 911 immediately. The San Marino Police Department or California Highway Patrol will respond to document the scene and create an official police report. This report is a vital piece of evidence.
3. **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Even if you feel fine, it is crucial to be evaluated by a medical professional. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries like concussions or internal bleeding. For severe trauma, you will likely be transported to a regional trauma center like **Huntington Hospital** in Pasadena. Other nearby facilities include **Methodist Hospital of Southern California** in Arcadia. A medical record creates a direct link between the accident and your injuries.
4. **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle damage, skid marks, traffic signals, and your injuries.
5. **Exchange Information:** Get the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details from all drivers involved. Also, collect contact information from any witnesses.
6. **Do Not Admit Fault:** Be polite, but do not apologize or accept blame for the accident. Stick to the facts when speaking with police and other parties. Any admission can be used against you by insurance companies.
7. **Contact a Personal Injury Attorney:** Before you speak with an insurance adjuster, call our firm. Insurance companies are focused on minimizing their payout, not on your well-being. We will protect your rights from the very beginning.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires a clear understanding of California's specific laws. Here are some key concepts that may affect your case:

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total compensation award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, you can still collect 80% of the damages.
* **Statute of Limitations:** In most personal injury cases, California law (CCP § 335.1) gives you **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit. However, there is a critical exception: if your claim is against a government entity (like a city, county, or state agency), you must file a formal claim within **six months** under the Government Claims Act. Missing these deadlines can permanently bar you from seeking compensation.
* **California's Minimum Insurance Requirements:** The state's minimum liability insurance coverage is just $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. In any serious accident, these amounts are woefully inadequate. An experienced attorney can help you explore other potential sources of recovery, such as your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
* **Types of Compensation:** A successful claim can help you recover compensation for both economic and non-economic losses. This can include medical expenses (past and future), lost wages, loss of earning capacity, property damage, pain and suffering, and emotional distress.

## What to Do After an Accident in Adelanto

The actions you take in the minutes and hours after an accident can significantly impact your health and your ability to recover financial compensation. If you are involved in a collision in Adelanto, try to remain calm and follow these steps:

1.  **Get to Safety:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic to a safe location. Turn on your hazard lights to warn other drivers. Check on yourself and your passengers for injuries.
2.  **Call 911:** Report the accident to the police immediately. Depending on your location, a deputy from the San Bernardino County Sheriff's Department or an officer from the California Highway Patrol (CHP) will respond. A police report is a crucial piece of evidence for your claim.
3.  **Seek Medical Attention:** This is the most important step. Because there are no hospitals directly within Adelanto city limits, paramedics will likely transport you to a nearby facility in Victorville or a trauma center in San Bernardino. Even if you feel fine, some serious injuries like concussions or internal bleeding may not have immediate symptoms. Seeing a doctor creates an official medical record of your injuries.
4.  **Document the Scene:** If it is safe to do so, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle damage, skid marks, road conditions, and any visible injuries.
5.  **Exchange Information:** Get the name, contact information, driver's license number, and insurance details from all other drivers involved. Also, try to get the names and phone numbers of any witnesses.
6.  **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid saying things like "I'm sorry" or "It was my fault." Stick to the facts when speaking with police and other drivers. Any admission of guilt can be used against you later by the insurance company.
7.  **Contact an Adelanto Personal Injury Lawyer:** Before you speak to an insurance adjuster, call Reardon Injury Law. We can protect you from lowball settlement offers and handle all communications on your behalf, allowing you to focus on your recovery.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system after an injury can be confusing. It is important to understand the key California laws that will affect your claim.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, your final compensation award will simply be reduced by 20%. Insurance companies often try to shift blame to victims to reduce their payout, which is why having an experienced attorney is so important.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** In California, you have a limited time to file a personal injury lawsuit. For most cases, the deadline is **two years** from the date of the injury (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, there is a critical exception: if your claim is against a government entity (e.g., a city bus, a county vehicle, or an accident caused by a poorly maintained public road), you must file a formal claim within **six months** under the Government Claims Act. Missing these deadlines can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. In a serious accident, these amounts are often not nearly enough to cover medical bills and lost wages. We will investigate all potential sources of recovery, including underinsured/uninsured motorist coverage and third-party liability.
*   **Available Compensation (Damages):** A successful personal injury claim can provide compensation for both economic and non-economic losses, including medical expenses (past and future), lost income, property damage, pain and suffering, and loss of enjoyment of life.

## What to Do After an Accident in Burlingame

The actions you take in the minutes and days following an accident are critical for protecting your health and your legal rights.

1.  **Ensure Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle to a safe location off the roadway. Check on others involved and call 911 immediately to report the accident and any injuries. The Burlingame Police Department or California Highway Patrol will respond and create an official report.
2.  **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Even if you feel fine, some serious injuries have delayed symptoms. Being evaluated at a local hospital like **Mills-Peninsula Medical Center** or **San Mateo Medical Center** creates a crucial medical record linking your injuries to the accident.
3.  **Document the Scene:** Use your phone to take pictures and videos of everything: vehicle positions, property damage, skid marks, traffic signals, weather conditions, and your visible injuries. If there are witnesses, ask for their names and contact information.
4.  **Exchange Information:** Exchange driver's license, contact, and insurance information with the other driver(s). Do not apologize or admit fault, as these statements can be used against you.
5.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you give a recorded statement to any insurance company—especially the at-fault party's—speak with an experienced personal injury lawyer. An attorney will protect you from tactics designed to devalue your claim and will begin building your case right away.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires an understanding of specific state laws. In California, several key rules will impact your case.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" system. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 10% responsible, you can still collect 90% of your damages.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** There are strict deadlines for filing a lawsuit. For most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a claim (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, the deadline is much shorter if a government entity is involved. If your injury was caused by a public entity like the City of Burlingame or a San Mateo County vehicle, you must file a formal claim within **six months** under the Government Claims Act.
*   **Compensation You Can Recover:** Victims are entitled to seek compensation for both economic and non-economic losses. This can include medical bills (past and future), lost wages, diminished earning capacity, property damage, pain and suffering, emotional distress, and loss of enjoyment of life.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's mandatory minimum liability insurance is only $15,000 per person, $30,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $5,000 for property damage. These low amounts are often exhausted quickly in a serious accident, making it vital to identify all possible sources of recovery, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist coverage.

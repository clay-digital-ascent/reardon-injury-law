## What to Do After an Accident in Hawthorne

The actions you take in the minutes and hours after an accident are crucial for protecting your health and your legal rights. Follow these steps:

1.  **Move to Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Call 911 immediately to report the accident and request police and medical assistance. The Hawthorne Police Department typically responds to accidents on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles incidents on the I-405 and other freeways.
2.  **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Accept paramedic assistance at the scene and agree to be transported to a hospital if recommended. For injuries, you may be taken to a local facility like **Hawthorne Community Medical Center**, the nearby **Centinela Hospital Medical Center** in Inglewood, or a major trauma center like **UCLA Medical Center** for severe cases. Even if you feel fine, see a doctor, as many serious injuries have delayed symptoms.
3.  **Document the Scene:** If you are physically able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of everything: vehicle positions, property damage, skid marks, traffic signals, and your injuries.
4.  **Exchange Information:** Get the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details from all drivers involved. Also, get the names and phone numbers of any witnesses.
5.  **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid saying "I'm sorry" or admitting any level of fault. Stick to the facts when speaking with the police. Do not give a recorded statement to the other driver's insurance adjuster before consulting an attorney.
6.  **Contact a Personal Injury Attorney:** An experienced lawyer can protect you from aggressive insurance adjusters and ensure all evidence is preserved. This allows you to focus completely on your recovery while we handle the legal process.

Your case will likely be filed at the **Inglewood Courthouse**, which serves Hawthorne residents for civil matters. Having a lawyer who understands the local court procedures is a significant advantage.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires a clear understanding of California's specific laws. Here are some key principles that will affect your case:

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California law allows you to recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, your final compensation award will simply be reduced by 20%. Insurance companies often try to exploit this rule by unfairly shifting blame onto the victim; we fight to ensure liability is assigned correctly.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** In most cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, a critical exception applies to claims against government entities (e.g., a city-operated bus, a dangerous public road). Under the California Government Claims Act, you must file a formal notice of claim within just **six months** of the incident. Missing this deadline can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
*   **California's Minimum Insurance Requirements:** The state-mandated minimum liability coverage is only $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. In any serious accident, these amounts are quickly exhausted. Our firm excels at identifying all possible sources of compensation, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage and any third parties who may share liability.
*   **Recoverable Damages:** As a victim, you are entitled to seek compensation for economic and non-economic losses, including past and future medical expenses, lost wages, diminished earning capacity, property damage, and pain and suffering.

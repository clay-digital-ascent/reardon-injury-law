## What to Do After an Accident in Newman

The moments after an accident are confusing and stressful. Taking the right steps can protect your health and your legal rights.

1. **Prioritize Safety and Get Help:** Move to a safe location if you can and call 911 immediately. Report the accident and any injuries. Depending on your location, the Newman Police Department or the California Highway Patrol (CHP) will respond.
2. **Seek Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Because Newman does not have its own hospital, paramedics will likely transport you to a nearby facility such as **Doctors Medical Center** in Modesto or **Memorial Hospital Los Banos**. Even if you feel fine, it is critical to get a medical evaluation. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries, and a medical record is essential for your injury claim.
3. **Document the Scene:** If you are able, use your phone to take pictures of the vehicles, the surrounding area, road conditions, and your injuries. Get the names and contact information of any witnesses.
4. **Exchange Information:** Exchange driver's licenses, insurance information, and contact details with the other driver(s). Do not apologize or admit fault.
5. **Contact an Attorney:** Before you give a recorded statement to an insurance adjuster, speak with a personal injury lawyer. An attorney can protect you from tactics used by insurance companies to devalue your claim and will handle all communications on your behalf.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system after an injury can be daunting. In California, several key laws will affect your case.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your total compensation will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 10% responsible, your final award will be reduced by 10%.
* **Statute of Limitations:** You have a limited time to take legal action. For most personal injury cases, you must file a lawsuit within **two years** of the date of the injury (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). This deadline is much shorter for claims against government entities. If your injury was caused by a public entity—like a city-owned vehicle or a dangerous condition on a public road—you must file a formal claim within **six months** under the Government Claims Act.
* **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low limits are often exhausted quickly in a serious accident, which is why it is crucial to explore all avenues for compensation.
* **Available Compensation:** As an injury victim, you may be entitled to recover compensation (known as "damages") for a wide range of losses, including past and future medical expenses, lost wages and earning capacity, property damage, and non-economic damages like pain, suffering, and emotional distress.

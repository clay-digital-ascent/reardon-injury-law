## What to Do After an Accident in San Juan Bautista

The steps you take immediately following an accident are critical for protecting both your health and your legal rights.

1.  **Get to Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Call 911 immediately. Report the accident and any injuries. For a crash on US 101 or SR 25, the California Highway Patrol (CHP) will likely respond. For incidents on city streets, it may be the San Benito County Sheriff's Office.
2.  **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Even if you feel you are "only shaken up," accept medical care from paramedics or go to an emergency room. For San Juan Bautista residents, this will likely mean traveling to a facility like **Hazel Hawkins Memorial Hospital in Hollister** or another regional trauma center in Salinas or Santa Cruz. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries, and creating an immediate medical record is crucial for your personal injury claim.
3.  **Document the Scene:** If you are physically able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle damage, skid marks, road conditions, and any visible injuries. Get the names and contact information of any witnesses.
4.  **Exchange Information:** Exchange driver's licenses, insurance information, and contact details with the other driver(s). Do not argue or apologize. Stick to the facts.
5.  **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid making statements like "I'm sorry" or "I think I looked away," as they can be used against you by insurance companies. Provide a factual statement to the police, but do not speculate or accept blame.
6.  **Contact an Attorney:** Before you give a recorded statement to an insurance adjuster, speak with a qualified personal injury attorney. The insurance company's goal is to minimize its payout. Your attorney's goal is to protect your rights and ensure you receive fair compensation.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating the legal system can be complex, but understanding a few key California laws is essential for any injury victim.

*   **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your total award will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 10% at fault, your compensation will be reduced by 10%.
*   **Statute of Limitations:** In most cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If you miss this deadline, you will likely lose your right to seek compensation forever.
*   **Claims Against Government Entities:** The deadline is much shorter if your injury was caused by a negligent public entity, such as a poorly maintained road owned by the county or a city-operated vehicle. Under the California Government Claims Act, you must file a formal claim with the responsible government agency within **six months** of the injury.
*   **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's mandatory minimum liability insurance is only $15,000 per person for bodily injury, $30,000 per accident, and $5,000 for property damage. These low limits are often insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury. An experienced attorney can help identify all potential sources of recovery, including your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UIM) coverage.
*   **Available Compensation:** As an injury victim, you may be entitled to recover damages for medical expenses (both past and future), lost wages, loss of future earning capacity, property damage, and non-economic damages like pain, suffering, and emotional distress.

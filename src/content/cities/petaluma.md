## What to Do After an Accident in Petaluma

The moments after an accident are chaotic and confusing. Taking the right steps can protect your health and your legal rights.

1. **Ensure Your Safety:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic to a safe location. Turn on your hazard lights to alert other drivers.
2. **Call 911:** Report the accident immediately. The Petaluma Police Department will likely respond to crashes on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles incidents on US-101. Request that a police report be filed and that paramedics are sent to the scene.
3. **Seek Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Even if you feel fine, some serious injuries have delayed symptoms. Allow paramedics to examine you and follow their advice. It is critical to get a full medical evaluation at an urgent care clinic or an emergency room. While Petaluma does not have a major trauma center, larger facilities like Santa Rosa Memorial Hospital are nearby to treat severe injuries.
4. **Document Everything:** Use your phone to take pictures of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, traffic signals, and any visible injuries. If there are witnesses, politely ask for their names and contact information.
5. **Exchange Information:** Exchange driver's license, contact, and insurance information with the other driver(s). Do not apologize or admit fault. Stick to the facts of what happened.
6. **Contact an Attorney:** Before you give a recorded statement to any insurance company—especially the other driver’s—speak with a personal injury lawyer. An attorney can advise you on your rights and protect you from tactics adjusters use to minimize your claim.

## What to Do After an Accident in Carlsbad

The steps you take immediately after an accident are crucial for protecting your health and your legal rights. Stay as calm as possible and follow this guide:

1. **Get to Safety:** If possible, move your vehicle out of traffic to a safe location. Turn on your hazard lights. Check on yourself and your passengers.
2. **Call 911:** Report the accident immediately. The Carlsbad Police Department will likely respond to incidents on city streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles crashes on I-5 and CA-78. A police report is vital evidence.
3. **Seek Medical Attention:** Even if you feel fine, accept medical assistance from paramedics. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. If necessary, you may be transported to a nearby trauma center, such as Tri-City Medical Center in Oceanside, for a full evaluation. A documented medical visit creates a direct link between the accident and your injuries.
4. **Document the Scene:** Use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, traffic signals, and any visible injuries.
5. **Exchange Information:** Get the names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details of all drivers involved. If there are witnesses, ask for their names and phone numbers.
6. **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid saying "I'm sorry" or accepting blame. Stick to the facts when speaking with police and other parties.
7. **Contact an Attorney:** Before you speak with an insurance adjuster, call Reardon Injury Law. Insurance companies aim to minimize payouts. Having an experienced attorney on your side from the beginning ensures your rights are protected and you don't accept a settlement that is far less than you deserve.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires an understanding of key California laws. Our team will guide you through every aspect, but here are some fundamental principles:

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total compensation award will simply be reduced by your percentage of fault.
* **Statute of Limitations:** In most cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). However, the deadline is much shorter for claims against government entities. If your injury was caused by a public entity—such as a poorly maintained road owned by the City of Carlsbad or a collision with a city vehicle—you must file a formal claim within **six months** under the Government Claims Act. Missing these deadlines can permanently bar you from recovering compensation.
* **California's Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California law only requires drivers to carry minimum liability coverage of $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. In a serious accident, these amounts are often woefully inadequate to cover medical bills and lost wages, which is why your own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UM/UIM) coverage can be so important.
* **Recoverable Damages:** A successful personal injury claim can help you recover compensation for economic damages (medical bills, future medical care, lost wages, property damage) and non-economic damages (pain, suffering, emotional distress, and loss of enjoyment of life).

If your case proceeds to litigation in Carlsbad, it will likely be filed and heard at the **North County Regional Center in Vista**, which is the branch of the San Diego Superior Court that handles civil matters for this region.

## What to Do After an Accident in Cerritos

The moments following an accident are chaotic and stressful. Taking the right steps can protect your health and your legal rights.

1. **Ensure Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move your vehicle out of traffic. Check on others involved. Call 911 immediately to report the accident and request emergency medical services. The Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department, which serves Cerritos, will likely respond to the scene.
2. **Seek Immediate Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Accept paramedic assistance and go to the emergency room if recommended. Even if you feel fine, it is crucial to get a full medical evaluation soon after the crash. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. There are several major medical centers in nearby Long Beach and Norwalk equipped to handle trauma.
3. **Document the Scene:** If you are physically able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, road conditions, traffic signals, and any visible injuries.
4. **Gather Information:** Exchange names, contact information, driver's license numbers, and insurance details with all other parties involved. Get contact information from any witnesses.
5. **Report the Accident, But Be Careful:** You must report the accident to your insurance company, but stick to the facts. Do not apologize, admit fault, or give a recorded statement to the other driver's insurance adjuster without first speaking to an attorney.
6. **Contact a Cerritos Personal Injury Lawyer:** An experienced attorney can handle communication with insurance companies, gather evidence, and ensure you meet all critical deadlines, allowing you to focus on your recovery.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires an understanding of key California laws.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" rule. This means you can still recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your final compensation will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, your recovery would be reduced by 20%.
* **Statute of Limitations:** In most personal injury cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If you miss this deadline, you will likely lose your right to seek compensation forever.
* **Claims Against Government Entities:** The deadline is much shorter if your claim is against a public entity, such as the City of Cerritos or a public bus system. You must file a formal claim with the government agency within **six months** of the injury.
* **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's minimum liability insurance requirement is only $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low limits are often insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury, making it vital to explore all options for recovery.
* **Available Compensation:** A successful personal injury claim can provide compensation for economic damages (like medical bills, lost wages, and future care costs) and non-economic damages (like pain, suffering, and emotional distress).

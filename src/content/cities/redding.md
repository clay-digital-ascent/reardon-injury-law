## Accident Hotspots in Redding

While any road can be dangerous, local and regional data highlight several major corridors in and around Redding that see a disproportionate number of serious and fatal accidents. The heavy volume of commercial and commuter traffic on these state highways contributes significantly to the risk. In fact, from 2010 to 2019, about 60% of the region’s traffic fatalities and severe injuries occurred on these routes.

Key areas of concern in the Redding area include:

* **Interstate 5 (I-5):** As the primary north-south artery on the West Coast, I-5 through Redding is a major trucking corridor. The mix of high-speed passenger vehicles and thousands of heavy trucks daily creates a high-risk environment for devastating <a href="/truck-accidents">truck accidents</a>, rear-end collisions, and multi-vehicle pile-ups.
* **State Route 44 (SR 44):** This heavily traveled east-west route serves as a key connector and another significant trucking path. Accidents on SR 44 are often caused by unsafe speeds, drivers following too closely, and poor visibility, leading to serious <a href="/car-accidents">car accidents</a> and commercial vehicle collisions.
* **State Route 299 (SR 299):** As a major east-west highway crossing Shasta County, SR 299 sees substantial regional traffic. Its road conditions and high traffic volume make it prone to head-on collisions and sideswipes, which can be particularly catastrophic.
* **State Route 273 (SR 273):** Running parallel to I-5, this local route is essential for commutes and business access. It has a high frequency of injury accidents, with rear-end collisions being the most common type of crash reported.

These routes are often the scene of serious <a href="/motorcycle-accidents">motorcycle accidents</a>, where riders are especially vulnerable, as well as incidents involving <a href="/pedestrian-accidents">pedestrian accidents</a> and <a href="/bicycle-accidents">bicycle accidents</a> at intersections and crosswalks.

## What to Do After an Accident in Redding

The steps you take immediately following an accident are critical for protecting your health and your legal rights.

1. **Ensure Safety and Call 911:** If you are able, move to a safe location away from traffic. Call 911 immediately to report the accident and request medical assistance for anyone who is injured. Depending on your location, the Redding Police Department or the California Highway Patrol (CHP) will respond.
2. **Seek Medical Attention:** Your health is the top priority. Accept paramedic assistance and go to the emergency room if necessary. For any accident-related injuries in Redding, you will likely be treated at **Mercy Medical Center Redding** or **Shasta Regional Medical Center**. Even if you feel fine, it is crucial to get a medical evaluation, as some serious injuries have delayed symptoms.
3. **Document the Scene:** If you are physically able, use your phone to take pictures and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle damage, skid marks, road conditions, and your injuries. If there were witnesses, get their names and contact information.
4. **Report the Accident:** Cooperate with the responding law enforcement officers. Provide a factual statement but avoid speculating or admitting fault. Be sure to ask for the report number and how you can obtain a copy of the official traffic collision report.
5. **Be Cautious with Insurance Companies:** You will likely be contacted by the at-fault party's insurance adjuster. Do not give a recorded statement or accept a quick settlement offer without first speaking to an attorney. Their goal is to minimize their payout, not to protect your best interests.
6. **Contact a Personal Injury Attorney:** An experienced lawyer can protect you from the insurance companies and ensure you take the right steps to build a strong claim.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires a clear understanding of California's specific laws. An attorney can help you apply these rules to your case.

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California follows a "pure comparative negligence" system. This means you can still recover financial compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your final award will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, your compensation will be reduced by 20%.
* **Statute of Limitations:** There are strict deadlines for filing a personal injury lawsuit. In California, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a claim (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). Critically, if your claim is against a government entity (e.g., a city bus, a poorly maintained public road), you have only **six months** to file a formal notice of claim under the Government Claims Act. Missing these deadlines can permanently bar you from seeking compensation.
* **California's Minimum Insurance Requirements:** The state requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of only $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low limits are often insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury. An experienced attorney can investigate all possible sources of recovery, including underinsured/uninsured motorist coverage and third-party liability.
* **Compensation You Can Recover:** A successful personal injury claim can provide compensation for both economic and non-economic damages, including medical bills (past and future), lost wages, loss of earning capacity, property damage, pain and suffering, and emotional distress.

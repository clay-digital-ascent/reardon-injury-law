## What to Do After an Accident in Hesperia

The steps you take immediately after an accident are crucial for protecting both your health and your legal rights. If you are involved in a crash in Hesperia, try to follow these steps:

1. **Prioritize Safety:** If you can, move your vehicle to the side of the road to avoid further collisions. Turn on your hazard lights. Check on yourself and your passengers for injuries.
2. **Contact Emergency Services:** Call 911 immediately. The San Bernardino County Sheriff's Department (Hesperia Station) typically responds to accidents on local streets, while the California Highway Patrol (CHP) handles incidents on I-15 and state routes. A police report is vital evidence for your claim.
3. **Seek Medical Attention:** Even if you feel fine, it's essential to get a medical evaluation. Adrenaline can mask serious injuries. Go to an emergency room at a nearby facility like **Desert Valley Hospital** or **Victor Valley Global Medical Center** in Victorville, or see your primary care physician as soon as possible. This creates an official record of your injuries.
4. **Document the Scene:** If you are able, take photos and videos of the accident scene, including vehicle damage, skid marks, road conditions, and any visible injuries. Exchange contact and insurance information with the other driver(s), but do not discuss fault.
5. **Do Not Admit Fault:** Avoid making statements like "I'm sorry" or "It was my fault." Stick to the facts when speaking with law enforcement. Any admission of guilt can be used against you by insurance companies.
6. **Consult with an Attorney:** Before you give a recorded statement to any insurance adjuster, contact Reardon Injury Law. We will protect you from tactics designed to devalue your claim and will handle all communications on your behalf.

## Understanding Your Rights Under California Law

Navigating a personal injury claim requires a clear understanding of California's specific laws. An experienced attorney can ensure your rights are protected throughout the process. Key laws that may affect your Hesperia injury claim include:

* **Pure Comparative Negligence:** California law allows you to recover damages even if you were partially at fault for the accident. Your total compensation will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found to be 20% at fault, you can still collect 80% of the damages.
* **Statute of Limitations:** In most cases, you have **two years** from the date of the injury to file a personal injury lawsuit (California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1). If you miss this deadline, you will likely lose your right to seek compensation forever.
* **Claims Against Government Entities:** The deadline is much shorter if your claim is against a public entity, such as the City of Hesperia or San Bernardino County (e.g., an accident involving a city vehicle or a dangerous road condition). You must file a formal notice of claim within **six months** of the incident.
* **Minimum Insurance Requirements:** California's minimum liability insurance coverage is just $15,000 for injury to one person, $30,000 for injury to multiple people, and $5,000 for property damage. These low limits are often insufficient to cover the costs of a serious injury. We are skilled at identifying all possible sources of compensation to ensure your needs are met.

You may be entitled to compensation for medical expenses (current and future), lost wages, loss of earning capacity, and non-economic damages like pain, suffering, and emotional distress.
